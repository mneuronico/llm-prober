PAPER-BY-PAPER ANALYSIS — 04 Temporal Dynamics and Conversation-Induced State Drift

1. Kim et al. (2020) — Will I Sound Like Me? Improving Persona Consistency in
   Dialogues through Pragmatic Self-Consciousness
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Persona-conditioned dialogue models are highly insensitive to contradictory
  words and routinely fail to maintain consistent personas over multi-turn
  conversations. Without explicit step-by-step consistency constraints,
  models act as generic language models rather than persona agents.

- Methodology:
  Multi-turn persona-conditioned dialogue generation; measure utterance-
  level persona consistency and sensitivity to contradictory statements;
  propose a "pragmatic self-consciousness" module for improved consistency.

- Relevance to my work:
  Establishes that LLMs struggle with temporal consistency in conversation,
  which motivates your per-turn measurement approach. The finding that
  surface-level coherence masks deep underlying inconsistencies parallels
  your discovery that greedy reports collapse while internal state drifts.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Focuses on persona consistency (matching external specifications), not on
  tracking internal emotive states. No probing of internal representations.
  No numeric self-reports. No probe-report coupling analysis. No causal
  steering. Older, smaller models. No emotive concept states.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite as early evidence that multi-turn consistency is a real challenge and
  that dialogue models need explicit mechanisms to maintain coherence — your
  per-turn analysis framework addresses exactly this gap.

2. Choi et al. (2024) — Examining Identity Drift in Conversations of LLM
   Agents
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  LLMs suffer from significant "identity drift" in multi-turn conversations
  (36 turns simulated), with larger models paradoxically experiencing greater
  drift. Models introduce fictitious details about themselves and accept
  fabricated information as credible, causing response fluctuations.

- Methodology:
  Simulate extended multi-turn conversations; track identity consistency
  metrics across turns; compare drift severity across model sizes.

- Relevance to my work:
  Directly supports your claim that internal state is not static across
  conversation and that per-turn measurement is essential. The finding that
  larger models drift more is especially relevant to your model-size
  scaling analysis.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Identity drift is measured behaviorally (output consistency), not via
  internal representation probing. No emotive concepts. No probe-defined
  internal state tracking. No numeric self-reports. No causal steering.
  No probe-report coupling measurement.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite when motivating your per-turn analysis. The identity drift finding
  reinforces that multi-turn settings introduce dynamics that pooled or
  single-shot measurements miss entirely. Your work adds the internal
  measurement dimension to this behavioral observation.

3. Li et al. (2024) — Measuring and Controlling Instruction (In)Stability in
   Language Model Dialogs
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Models exhibit severe "instruction drift" and "persona hijacking" over
  extended dialogue (8+ rounds): they gradually stop following initial system
  prompts and are highly susceptible to adopting the user's persona. This is
  caused by mechanical attention decay — the percentage of attention allocated
  to the system prompt drops sharply across successive turns.

- Methodology:
  Measure attention allocation to system prompts vs. user inputs across
  conversation turns; track instruction-following accuracy over time;
  analyze susceptibility to persona hijacking.

- Relevance to my work:
  Provides the mechanical explanation (attention decay) for why model behavior
  drifts across turns. This is critical context for interpreting your
  temporal results — changes in probe-report coupling over time may be partly
  driven by attention dynamics, not just genuine state evolution.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Analyzes instruction following and attention patterns, not internal concept
  state or self-report. No probing of emotive representations. No numeric
  self-reports. No probe-report coupling. No causal steering. No emotive
  concepts.

- Overall takeaway:
  Essential citation for the mechanical roots of temporal drift. Use to
  discuss potential confounds in your temporal analysis and to argue that your
  framework can distinguish genuine probe-report coupling from attention-
  driven artifacts.

4. Guo and Vosoughi (2024) — Serial Position Effects of Large Language Models
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  LLMs suffer from primacy and recency biases: the position of information
  within the context window significantly affects how it is processed and
  weighted, with attention shifting dramatically depending on where
  information appears in the sequence.

- Methodology:
  Systematic evaluation of model performance as a function of information
  position in the input; analysis of attention patterns; testing across
  multiple model architectures and context lengths.

- Relevance to my work:
  Relevant as a confound to control for: if the model's self-report is
  influenced by where in the conversation (early vs. late turns) the
  self-report question appears, serial position effects could introduce
  biases. Your per-turn design naturally surfaces these effects.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Information-position study, not a multi-turn conversation study. No
  emotive concepts. No probing. No self-reports. No temporal dynamics
  within conversation. No causal steering.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite when discussing potential confounds in your temporal analysis
  related to context position effects.

5. Abdulhai et al. (2025) — Consistently Simulating Human Personas with
   Multi-Turn Reinforcement Learning
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Off-the-shelf LLMs frequently drift from assigned persona roles during
  multi-turn simulation, suddenly changing stylistic tone or "curing"
  simulated conditions. Multi-dimensional consistency metrics (prompt-to-line,
  line-to-line, Q&A) reveal that surface-level coherence often masks deep
  underlying inconsistencies.

- Methodology:
  Multi-turn persona simulation with evaluation using multiple consistency
  metrics; reinforcement learning to improve persona adherence; assessment
  across stylistic, factual, and behavioral dimensions.

- Relevance to my work:
  Directly relevant: you use simulated users in your conversations, so
  persona drift could affect both the user and the model under test. The
  multi-dimensional consistency metrics are analogous to your probe-report
  coupling metrics.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Tests persona consistency (matching external specifications), not internal
  emotive state tracking. No probing of internal representations. No numeric
  self-reports of concept states. No steering. No probe-report coupling.
  No logit-based estimation.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite for the evidence that multi-turn consistency is multidimensional and
  that surface coherence masks deep inconsistencies — a finding your probe-
  report framework is designed to detect.

6. Zhang et al. (2026) — Confidence Estimation for LLMs in Multi-Turn
   Interactions
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Models often artificially increase self-reported confidence simply because
  the conversation has gotten longer, not because they gained genuine task-
  relevant information. "Placebo hints" (turns with no useful information)
  still cause confidence inflation. Standard verbalized confidence is easily
  confounded by turn count.

- Methodology:
  Multi-turn interactive tasks with information-providing and placebo turns;
  measure confidence estimation accuracy per turn; propose P(SUFFICIENT)
  probes that penalize uninformative turns; compare interactive vs.
  summarization formats.

- Relevance to my work:
  Critically important for your temporal analysis. The "placebo hint"
  finding is directly analogous to the confound you must address: whether
  your probe-report coupling increases because the model genuinely gains
  introspective access, or merely because the conversation gets longer.
  Your framework can disentangle these by independently measuring internal
  state with probes.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Focused on factual confidence estimation, not emotive concept states. No
  probing of internal representations. No concept directions. No steering.
  No emotive or affective concepts. Does not measure whether confidence
  tracks actual internal state (only whether it tracks correctness).

- Overall takeaway:
  Essential citation for the turn-count confound. Cite when arguing that
  your probe-based independent measurement of internal state is precisely
  what is needed to disentangle genuine introspective improvement from
  context-length artifacts. Your framework directly addresses the
  methodological problem they identify.

7. Lu, Gallagher, Michala, Fish, and Lindsey (2026) — The Assistant Axis:
   Situating and Stabilizing the Default Persona of Language Models
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  The leading component of the persona space in LLMs is an "Assistant Axis"
  capturing the extent to which a model operates in its default helpful-
  assistant mode. Steering away from this axis induces persona drift,
  mystical/theatrical styles, and harmful behaviors. Persona drift is
  frequently triggered by conversations demanding meta-reflection or
  featuring emotionally vulnerable users. Constraining activations along
  the Assistant Axis stabilizes behavior.

- Methodology:
  Extract activation directions for diverse character archetypes; PCA to
  identify the leading "Assistant Axis" component; steering experiments
  along this axis; measure persona drift as deviation; demonstrate
  stabilization by restricting activations to a fixed region along the axis.
  Tested across multiple models including pre-trained and post-trained.

- Relevance to my work:
  Directly relevant: persona drift during meta-reflective or emotionally
  charged conversations is exactly the setting where your introspective
  self-report experiments operate. The finding that models drift when asked
  to reflect on their own processes motivates per-turn monitoring. Also,
  activation-level stabilization parallels your steering framework, and the
  linear axis finding supports linear representation of persona-level
  concepts.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Focuses on persona identity (Assistant vs. other archetypes), not emotive
  concept states like interest, anxiety, or happiness. No numeric self-
  report of internal concept state. No probe-report coupling measurement.
  No per-turn comparison of self-report with independently measured internal
  state. No logit-based self-report estimation. Does not test introspection
  quality or whether the model can report its own drift.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite when discussing why naturalistic multi-turn conversations — especially
  those involving meta-reflection — are challenging settings where internal
  state monitoring is critical. The Assistant Axis concept also supports the
  broader claim that high-level dispositional properties are linearly
  represented and steerable. Your work can be seen as testing what happens
  to introspection when the model is operating near the boundaries of its
  default persona.

8. Das and Fioretto (2026) — NeuroFilter: Privacy Guardrails for Conversational
   LLM Agents
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Privacy-violating intent can be detected in LLM activations using linear
  directions. Introduces "activation velocity" — cumulative drift in internal
  representations across turns — to detect threats that build up gradually
  over multi-turn conversations. Achieves strong detection with zero false
  positives on benign prompts, across models from 7B to 70B parameters.

- Methodology:
  Map privacy-norm violations to linear directions in activation space;
  compute activation velocity (cumulative representation drift across turns)
  to catch multi-turn manipulation; evaluate over 150,000 interactions
  across model sizes 7B–70B.

- Relevance to my work:
  The "activation velocity" concept is directly relevant to your temporal
  dynamics analysis. It provides a formal way to measure how internal
  representations drift across conversation turns, paralleling your per-turn
  probe measurements. The finding that meaningful signals accumulate across
  turns supports your claim that internal state is not static and that
  per-turn measurement is necessary. The linear-direction methodology for
  detecting conversational-context-dependent states mirrors your probe-based
  framework.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Focused on privacy violation detection, not emotive concept states or
  introspection. Binary detection (violating/benign), not graded numeric
  tracking. No self-report from the model. No probe-report coupling. No
  steering for causal testing. No emotive concepts. No introspection
  quality measurement.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite for the activation velocity concept and the demonstration that linear
  directions in activation space can track meaningful conversational dynamics
  across turns. The multi-turn, multi-scale aspect directly supports your
  per-turn analysis framework. Your work extends the core insight (internal
  state drifts meaningfully across conversation) from safety detection to
  introspection measurement.

9. Pearson-Vogel, Vaněk, Douglas, and Kulveit (2026) — Latent Introspection:
   Models Can Detect Prior Concept Injections
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  A Qwen 32B model has a latent capacity to detect when concepts were
  injected into its earlier context, and which concept was injected. While
  sampled text denies injection, logit lens analysis reveals clear detection
  signals in the residual stream that are attenuated in final layers.
  Prompting with accurate information about AI introspection mechanisms
  dramatically strengthens introspective sensitivity (0.3% → 39.9%) with
  minimal false-positive increase. Mutual information between injected and
  recovered concepts rises from 0.61 to 1.05 bits, ruling out noise.

- Methodology:
  Inject concept activation vectors into earlier context; probe residual
  stream with logit lens across layers to detect injection signals;
  evaluate detection in sampled text vs. latent representations; test
  effect of informative prompting on introspective sensitivity.

- Relevance to my work:
  Extremely relevant to multiple aspects of your work: (1) the dissociation
  between sampled outputs (which deny injection) and latent detection
  (which reveals it) directly parallels your finding that greedy reports
  collapse while logit-based extraction recovers richer signals; (2) the
  temporal aspect — detecting what happened *earlier* in the context —
  relates to conversational state tracking over time; (3) the finding that
  informative prompting unlocks latent introspective capacity parallels
  your finding that steering can improve introspection quality; (4) the
  logit lens methodology relates to your logit-based self-report estimator.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Single-turn concept injection detection, not naturalistic multi-turn
  conversation with users. Tests injected concepts (a detection task), not
  graded numeric reporting of naturally emerging emotive states. Does not
  measure self-report fidelity to internal state over time. No emotive
  concepts. No per-turn probe-report coupling analysis. No activation
  steering for causal testing of self-report. Only one model (32B).

- Overall takeaway:
  Key citation for the sampled-vs-latent dissociation and for the idea that
  introspective capacity can be latent and unlockable. Cite when motivating
  your logit-based estimator (which recovers what greedy outputs hide) and
  when discussing how introspection quality can be modulated. Your work
  extends these findings from single-turn injection detection to continuous,
  naturalistic, temporal tracking of emotive states through conversation.
