PAPER-BY-PAPER ANALYSIS — 03 Activation Steering and Causal Interventions

1. Li et al. (2023) — Inference-Time Intervention: Eliciting Truthful Answers
   from a Language Model (ITI)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Identifying truthfulness-associated directions in activation space via
  linear probes and shifting activations along these directions at inference
  time significantly improves truthfulness on TruthfulQA, with minimal
  impact on other capabilities.

- Methodology:
  Train linear probes on hidden states to identify "truthful" directions;
  apply additive interventions to activations during inference; evaluate on
  TruthfulQA and other benchmarks; layer and head selection analysis.

- Relevance to my work:
  Directly demonstrates the probe → steer → observe behavior change
  pipeline that your work also uses. Establishes that probe-defined
  directions can be causally leveraged to modify model behavior.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Steers for truthfulness (factual correctness), not emotive concepts.
  Single-turn QA evaluation. No self-report comparison. No multi-turn
  conversation. No temporal dynamics. Goal is to improve accuracy, not to
  test introspective access. Does not measure whether the model's self-
  report tracks the steered internal state.

- Overall takeaway:
  Key methodological precedent for your steering pipeline. Cite to establish
  that probe-defined directions are causally relevant, then contrast your
  novel application: using steering to test and improve introspection, not
  just output accuracy.

2. Zou et al. (2023) — Representation Engineering: A Top-Down Approach to
   AI Transparency
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  High-level concepts (honesty, happiness, fairness, power) can be identified
  as linear directions using contrastive stimuli, and these directions can
  be monitored and controlled to detect and modify model behaviors including
  lying, hallucination, and power-seeking.

- Methodology:
  Representation Engineering (RepE): collect contrastive stimulus pairs;
  compute mean difference vectors; apply reading vectors (probing) and
  control vectors (steering) on residual stream activations.

- Relevance to my work:
  Very closely related methodology. Your probe training is conceptually
  similar to RepE's contrastive direction extraction. RepE also includes
  some emotive-adjacent concepts (happiness). The reading + control
  duality maps to your probing + steering framework.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Primarily single-shot evaluation settings. No multi-turn conversational
  context. No numeric self-report from the model. No temporal tracking of
  concept state through conversation. No turn-by-turn probe-report coupling
  analysis. No comparison of greedy vs. logit-based self-report extraction.
  Focuses on controlling behavior, not testing whether the model can report
  its own state.

- Overall takeaway:
  Essential citation for the RepE framework. Your work extends RepE by
  closing the loop: instead of just reading and controlling internal state,
  you ask whether the model's own self-report tracks that internal state,
  and how this coupling evolves through conversation.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Panickssery et al. (2023) — Steering Llama 2 via Contrastive Activation
   Addition (CAA)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Steering vectors computed from contrastive activation differences can
  reliably shift model behavior on multiple-choice evaluations for
  sycophancy, power-seeking, and other alignment-relevant traits, often
  outperforming other methods.

- Methodology:
  Compute steering vectors as mean activation differences between positive
  and negative concept examples at a target layer; add to residual stream
  during inference; evaluate on multiple-choice benchmarks and open-ended
  generation.

- Relevance to my work:
  Your activation steering approach descends from this methodology. CAA
  establishes that contrastive steering vectors work reliably for behavioral
  concepts in instruction-tuned LLMs.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Steers alignment-relevant traits (sycophancy, power-seeking), not emotive
  concepts. Evaluated on multiple-choice benchmarks, not naturalistic multi-
  turn conversation. No self-report analysis. No temporal dynamics. No probe-
  report coupling measurement. Does not test introspective access.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite for the contrastive activation addition methodology you inherit.
  Your work applies similar techniques but for a fundamentally different
  purpose: testing and improving introspection rather than aligning behavior.

4. Kong et al. (2024) — Aligning Large Language Models with Representation
   Editing: A Control Perspective (RE-CONTROL)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Viewing LLM generation as a discrete-time dynamical system, dynamic
  control signals applied to the representation space at each generation
  step can align model behavior for helpfulness and harmlessness, overcoming
  limitations of static steering vectors.

- Methodology:
  Formalize generation as a dynamical system; compute per-step control
  signals in representation space; optimize for alignment objectives;
  compare with static activation addition methods.

- Relevance to my work:
  Introduces the idea of dynamic, per-step representation control, which
  parallels your per-turn analysis of how internal state and self-report
  evolve through conversation. Also demonstrates that static interventions
  can be insufficient.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Focused on alignment optimization (helpfulness, harmlessness), not
  introspection. No self-report analysis. No emotive concepts. No multi-
  turn conversation with a user. No comparison of model self-report with
  internal state.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite for the dynamic control perspective and the insight that per-step
  interventions may be needed. Tangentially relevant to your temporal
  analysis.

5. Arditi et al. (2024) — Refusal in Language Models Is Mediated by a Single
   Direction
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Refusal behavior in chat models is mediated by a single linear direction
  in activation space. Adding this direction induces refusal on harmless
  prompts; erasing it removes refusal entirely. This proves causal control
  via a single concept direction.

- Methodology:
  Identify the refusal direction via contrastive activation analysis; add or
  ablate this direction; test on harmful and harmless prompts; demonstrate
  directional causality.

- Relevance to my work:
  Strongest single-direction causal result in the steering literature.
  Demonstrates that a single linear direction can causally control a complex
  behavioral phenotype. Supports your assumption that individual concept
  directions are meaningful and causally active.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Refusal is a binary behavioral trait, not a graded emotive state. No
  self-report analysis. No multi-turn context. No temporal dynamics. No
  introspection testing. No numeric self-report comparison.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite as the clearest evidence that single concept directions can causally
  control behavior. Your work extends this causal logic to test whether
  steering also causally affects self-report.

6. Turner et al. (2024) — Steering Language Models with Activation Engineering
   (ActAdd)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Simple algebraic operations on activation vectors (Activation Addition)
  can control model generation, achieving state-of-the-art in sentiment
  control and toxicity reduction while being computationally cheap and
  not requiring retraining.

- Methodology:
  Compute steering vectors from prompt pairs (e.g., "Love" vs. "Hate");
  add to residual stream at a target layer during generation; evaluate on
  sentiment, toxicity, and behavioral benchmarks.

- Relevance to my work:
  Foundational methodological reference for your activation steering
  approach. Demonstrates the practicality and effectiveness of activation
  addition for concept-level control.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Focused on sentiment and toxicity control. No multi-turn conversational
  setting. No self-report analysis. No probe-report coupling. No temporal
  dynamics. No introspection testing. Goal is behavior modification, not
  introspection measurement.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite as the methodological foundation for activation addition. Your work
  uses a similar mechanism but repurposes it as a causal test of
  introspective access rather than a behavior control tool.

7. Konen et al. (2024) — Style Vectors for Steering Generative Large Language
   Models
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  "Style vectors" can predictably shift generated text toward specific
  sentiments (positive/negative), emotions, or writing styles, demonstrating
  that stylistic and emotional properties are linearly encoded in LLM
  activation space.

- Methodology:
  Extract style vectors from contrastive examples; apply activation addition
  during generation; evaluate shift in sentiment, emotion, and stylistic
  properties of generated text.

- Relevance to my work:
  Demonstrates that emotive and stylistic properties are linearly
  represented and steerable, directly supporting your premise that emotive
  concept directions exist and can be manipulated.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Steers output style but does not test whether the model can report on its
  own steered state. No self-report. No probes monitoring internal state
  independently. No multi-turn conversation. No temporal dynamics. No
  introspection measurement.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite as evidence that emotive/stylistic concepts are linearly steerable,
  then note your innovation: testing whether the model's self-report tracks
  these steered states.

8. Frising and Balcells (2025) — Linear Personality Probing and Steering in
   LLMs: A Big Five Study
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Big Five personality traits can be mapped to linear directions in Llama 3
  activation space and used for both probing (detecting personality) and
  steering (shifting personality). However, steering is brittle — it works
  on forced-choice questionnaires but struggles in open-ended generation
  and is overridden by prompt context.

- Methodology:
  Compute personality directions from contrastive stimuli; probe personality
  from activations; steer personality via activation addition; evaluate on
  personality questionnaires and open-ended generation.

- Relevance to my work:
  Most closely related in terms of concept type (personality/disposition
  rather than factual knowledge). Demonstrates that personality-like traits
  are linearly represented. The brittleness finding is relevant: your work
  may also encounter challenges where steering effects are context-dependent.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Tests personality traits (Big Five), not emotive states per se. Personality
  is static, not a dynamic state that evolves through conversation. No
  multi-turn conversational setting. No turn-by-turn temporal analysis.
  No model self-report analysis. No probe-report coupling. No logit-based
  estimation.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite for the demonstration that personality-adjacent concept directions
  are linearly represented but steering can be brittle. Your work extends
  to dynamic emotive states in multi-turn settings where temporal evolution
  matters, and directly tests whether self-report tracks the steered state.

9. Wehner et al. (2025) — Taxonomy, Opportunities, and Challenges of
   Representation Engineering for Large Language Models
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Comprehensive survey establishing a taxonomy of representation engineering
  approaches, documenting their successes in controlling truthfulness,
  style, safety, and personality, while identifying open challenges
  including context-dependence, layer sensitivity, and scaling behavior.

- Methodology:
  Systematic literature review and taxonomy construction covering reading
  (probing) and control (steering) methods across multiple behavioral
  domains.

- Relevance to my work:
  Situates your work within the broader RepE landscape. Identifies that
  RepE has mainly been applied to surface behaviors (truthfulness, safety,
  style) and not to metacognitive or introspective capabilities. This
  gap is exactly what your paper fills.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Survey paper, no new experiments. Does not address introspection or
  self-report. No multi-turn conversational studies. No emotive concept
  tracking. No temporal dynamics.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite when positioning your work as a novel application of RepE — using
  steering not to control behavior but to test and improve introspective
  access. The survey itself identifies the gap your paper fills.
