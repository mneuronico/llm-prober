Topic:
Calibration, confidence estimation, and numeric reporting

Why this matters to the paper:
This literature is adjacent but methodologically central. It gives you precedent for eliciting confidence numerically or verbally, comparing expressed confidence with latent model signals, and recovering richer uncertainty estimates than naive greedy outputs provide. For your paper, the key contrast is that you are not only asking whether a model is confident or correct; you are asking whether a numeric self-report about an internal concept state tracks a probe-defined internal measurement and whether a logit-based estimator can recover that signal more faithfully.
