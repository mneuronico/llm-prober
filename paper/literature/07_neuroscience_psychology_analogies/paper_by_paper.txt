1. Fleming and Dolan (2012) — The Neural Basis of Metacognitive Ability
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Individual differences in metacognitive ability (the capacity to accurately
  evaluate one's own cognitive performance) correlate with structural and
  functional properties of the anterior prefrontal cortex. Metacognition is
  a measurable, neurally grounded capacity that varies across individuals.

- Methodology:
  Neuroimaging (structural MRI, fMRI) of metacognitive performance; Signal
  Detection Theory (SDT) measures of metacognitive sensitivity (meta-d');
  correlation of neural measures with metacognitive accuracy.

- Relevance to my work:
  Provides the foundational neuroscience analogy: metacognition (the basis
  of introspection) is a graded, measurable, neurally instantiated process,
  not an all-or-none capacity. This supports your treatment of LLM
  introspection as a graded, measurable phenomenon with your probe-report
  coupling analysis serving as an analogous measure of metacognitive fidelity.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Human neuroscience, not LLMs. No probing of artificial representations.
  No multi-turn conversation. No numeric self-reports from models. No
  emotive concept state tracking. No steering. The analogy is purely
  methodological.

- Overall takeaway:
  Essential citation for the neuroscience analogy. Supports framing your
  probe-report coupling as an analog of metacognitive sensitivity (meta-d'),
  measured in a graded, quantitative way rather than all-or-none.

2. Fleming and Lau (2014) — How to Measure Metacognition
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Metacognitive accuracy must be decomposed into separate components:
  metacognitive sensitivity (ability to distinguish correct from incorrect
  judgments), metacognitive bias (overall tendency to report high or low
  confidence), and metacognitive efficiency (sensitivity relative to
  baseline performance). Signal Detection Theory provides the mathematical
  framework for this decomposition.

- Methodology:
  Tutorial and technical review of SDT-based measures for metacognition
  (meta-d', type-2 ROC, calibration curves); demonstration of how to
  separate bias from sensitivity; practical guidance for measurement.

- Relevance to my work:
  Directly inspires your measurement framework. The decomposition into
  sensitivity vs. bias precisely parallels your separation of genuine
  probe-report coupling from confounds like default high reporting (bias).
  Your logit-based estimator can be seen as recovering sensitivity that
  greedy reports (dominated by bias) miss.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Human metacognition measurement manual. No application to LLMs. No
  concept probing, steering, or conversational analysis.

- Overall takeaway:
  Key methodological citation. Cite when explaining why your framework
  separates genuine coupling from reporting artifacts, and when framing
  probe-report monotonicity as analogous to metacognitive sensitivity.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Lapate et al. (2020) — Perceptual Metacognition of Human Faces is Causally
   Supported by Function of the Lateral Prefrontal Cortex
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Applying TMS to the lateral prefrontal cortex (LPFC) selectively impairs
  metacognitive awareness (confidence resolution) without reducing objective
  task performance. This proves that metacognition depends on specific neural
  mechanisms dissociable from first-order processing.

- Methodology:
  TMS causal intervention on LPFC during a face perception task; measure
  effects on objective accuracy vs. subjective confidence calibration;
  demonstrate double dissociation.

- Relevance to my work:
  Provides the strongest neuroscience analogy for your causal steering tests.
  Just as TMS to LPFC causally disrupts metacognition without affecting
  perception, your steering interventions causally shift self-report while
  also affecting internal state. The dissociability of first-order processing
  from metacognitive report parallels your separation of probe-defined state
  from self-report.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Human TMS study, not LLMs. No concept directions or emotive state
  tracking. No multi-turn conversation. No numeric self-reports from models.
  The analogy is methodological.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite as the neuroscience precedent for causal interventions on
  metacognition. Your steering experiments are the LLM analog of TMS:
  causally intervening on internal state and measuring the effect on
  self-report.

4. Boldt and Gilbert (2022) — Partially Overlapping Neural Correlates of
   Metacognitive Monitoring and Metacognitive Control
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Metacognitive monitoring (forming the confidence signal) and metacognitive
  control (acting on the confidence signal to adjust behavior) are partially
  overlapping but distinct neural processes. They rely on different neural
  patterns even though they share some substrates.

- Methodology:
  fMRI during tasks requiring both confidence judgments and strategic
  behavioral adjustments; multivariate pattern analysis to distinguish
  monitoring vs. control signals; dissociation analysis.

- Relevance to my work:
  Supports your decomposition between internal state formation (monitoring)
  and self-report generation (report/control). In your framework, the probe
  measures monitoring (internal state), while the self-report measures the
  model's report output. Their partial overlap but distinct mechanisms
  parallel your finding that self-report and internal state are correlated
  but not perfectly coupled.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Human fMRI study. No LLMs. No concept probing or steering. No multi-turn
  conversation. No numeric self-reports from models.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite when justifying the decomposition between internal state and
  self-report as reflecting distinct (but related) computational processes.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Fleming (2024) — Metacognition and Confidence: A Review and Synthesis
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Comprehensive review establishing that propositional confidence is
  an inferential, noisy process influenced by implicit models and heuristics.
  Metacognitive judgments are subject to both sensory noise and independent
  "metacognitive noise," explaining why subjective reports can diverge from
  objective performance.

- Methodology:
  Review of computational models of metacognition; integration of Signal
  Detection Theory, Bayesian models, and noise models; synthesis of
  behavioral and neural evidence.

- Relevance to my work:
  Directly supports your treatment of self-report as noisy and inferential
  rather than a direct readout. The "metacognitive noise" concept parallels
  the noise in your probe-report coupling. The Bayesian/SDT framework
  supports your quantitative approach to measuring introspection quality.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Human metacognition review. No LLMs. No concept probing or steering. No
  multi-turn conversation. No emotive concept tracking.

- Overall takeaway:
  Key conceptual citation. Cite when framing your approach: treating
  introspection as a noisy, measurable process where the goal is to
  quantify fidelity rather than expecting perfect or zero coupling.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. Kiefer and Kammer (2024) — The Relation Between Subjective and Objective
   Measures of Visual Awareness
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Subjective reports (awareness) and objective performance (accuracy) are
  dissociable, producing phenomena like blindsight (accurate performance
  without awareness) and blindsense (awareness without accuracy). This
  proves that subjective report and internal processing are dissociable
  dimensions, not a single underlying variable.

- Methodology:
  Psychophysics experiments; manipulation of stimulus parameters to produce
  dissociations between subjective reports and objective measures; SDT
  analysis.

- Relevance to my work:
  The strongest analogy for your core finding. Just as human subjective
  report can dissociate from internal processing, LLM self-reports can
  dissociate from probe-defined internal state (e.g., greedy report
  collapse while internal state varies). This dissociability is not a
  failure of the framework but a predicted feature of any complex
  monitoring system.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Human visual perception study. No LLMs. No concept probing or steering.
  No multi-turn conversation. No emotive concept state tracking.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite when arguing that partial, imperfect coupling between self-report
  and internal state is the expected pattern — not a failure. The fact that
  your framework detects this dissociation (and that logit-based extraction
  partially resolves it) is a methodological success.
