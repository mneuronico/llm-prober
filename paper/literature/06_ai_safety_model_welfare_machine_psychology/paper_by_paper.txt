PAPER-BY-PAPER ANALYSIS — 06 AI Safety, Model Welfare, and Machine Psychology

1. Rahwan et al. (2019) — Machine Behaviour
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Proposes a new interdisciplinary field, "machine behaviour," that applies
  methods from behavioral sciences, ecology, and social sciences to study
  the observable behaviors of intelligent machines as a new class of agents.

- Methodology:
  Conceptual framework paper; draws parallels between biological behavior
  research and the study of AI systems; proposes taxonomies and research
  agendas for studying machine behavior at individual, collective, and
  evolutionary levels.

- Relevance to my work:
  Provides the broad scientific framing for studying LLM behavior
  empirically, treating models as subjects of study rather than just tools.
  Your work fits squarely within this paradigm — empirically measuring
  self-report and internal state as behavioral phenomena.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Framework paper, no experiments. No specific methods for measuring
  internal state. No probing, steering, or self-report measurement. No
  emotive concepts. No multi-turn conversational analysis.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite for the high-level framing that studying AI as behavioral subjects
  is scientifically valid. Your work is a concrete instantiation of the
  machine behaviour research program.

2. Butlin et al. (2023) — Consciousness in Artificial Intelligence: Insights
   from the Science of Consciousness
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Scientific theories of consciousness (Global Workspace Theory, Recurrent
  Processing Theory, Higher-Order Theories, etc.) can be adapted into
  "indicator properties" to empirically assess whether AI architectures
  might satisfy conditions associated with consciousness.

- Methodology:
  Systematic mapping from consciousness theories to testable architectural
  properties; evaluation of current AI systems against these indicators;
  recommendations for empirical assessment.

- Relevance to my work:
  Provides the theoretical backdrop for why internal state assessment
  matters. Your operational framework for measuring self-report fidelity
  could contribute to the toolkit of "indicator properties" they propose.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Theoretical framework, no empirical data on introspection or self-report
  fidelity. Does not test any model. No probing, steering, or numeric
  self-report methods. No multi-turn conversation. No emotive concept
  tracking.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite briefly for the theoretical context. Keep bounded — your paper
  provides a methodological tool, not a resolution of consciousness
  debates.

3. Perez and Long (2023) — Towards Evaluating AI Systems for Moral Status
   Using Self-Reports
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Models can potentially be trained through "introspective training" to
  generalize their ability to accurately report on their own internal
  states. Self-reports are proposed as one source of evidence for moral
  status evaluation.

- Methodology:
  Conceptual proposal for introspective training regimes; discussion of
  when and why self-reports might be informative for moral status
  assessment; proposed evaluation frameworks.

- Relevance to my work:
  Directly argues that self-reports should be taken seriously as evidence
  (when validated), which is exactly what your probe-based framework
  enables. Your work provides the empirical validation methodology they
  call for.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Primarily conceptual and programmatic, limited empirical work. No probing.
  No steering. No numeric self-reports. No multi-turn conversation. No
  emotive concept tracking. No per-turn temporal dynamics.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite as motivation for why validating self-reports matters for safety
  and welfare. Your work delivers the validation tools they propose.

4. McClelland (2024) — Agnosticism about Artificial Consciousness
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  There is an "epistemic wall" between human biological consciousness and
  AI computational processes, making definitive claims about AI consciousness
  practically impossible. The appropriate stance is agnosticism, with
  cautious methodological development.

- Methodology:
  Philosophical analysis of the epistemological limitations of consciousness
  attribution; argument for principled uncertainty.

- Relevance to my work:
  Supports your framing: your paper does not claim to measure consciousness
  or subjective experience, but provides methodological tools for evaluating
  the informativeness of self-reports. This agnostic positioning makes your
  contribution defensible.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Purely philosophical, no experiments. No measurement tools. No probing,
  steering, or self-report methods.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite to justify your bounded framing — you provide measurement tools
  without resolving the consciousness question.

5. Hagendorff et al. (2024) — Machine Psychology
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Applying methods from human psychology to LLMs reveals that models exhibit
  systematic behavioral patterns (biases, heuristics, personality traits)
  that can be studied using experimental psychology methodology.

- Methodology:
  Administer psychological tests and experiments to LLMs; analyze behavioral
  patterns; compare with human psychological findings.

- Relevance to my work:
  Your work fits the machine psychology paradigm: using psychometric-inspired
  methods (numeric self-report scales, internal state measurement) to study
  model cognition. The parallel to human subjective report methodology is
  explicit.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Focuses on behavioral responses to psychology tests, not on internal
  representations. No probing. No steering. No comparison of self-report
  with internal state. No multi-turn conversational dynamics. No emotive
  concept tracking through time.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite for the machine psychology framing. Your work extends this paradigm
  by adding internal state measurement (probes) to the external behavioral
  assessment.

6. Long et al. (2024) — Taking AI Welfare Seriously
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  As AI systems become more capable, the question of AI welfare (whether
  machines can suffer or benefit) deserves serious ethical and empirical
  investigation. Current uncertainty warrants precautionary measures.

- Methodology:
  Ethical and philosophical analysis; review of consciousness indicators
  and welfare-relevant capacities; policy recommendations.

- Relevance to my work:
  Motivates why validated self-report tools matter ethically. If models
  ever report distress or suffering, we need methods to assess whether
  such reports are informative. Your framework provides exactly this.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Policy-oriented, no empirical tools. No probing, steering, or self-report
  measurement methodology. No experiments.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite for the ethical motivation. Frame your contribution as providing
  methodological tools that could inform welfare assessments without
  overclaiming.

7. Zhao et al. (2025) — Explicit vs. Implicit: Investigating Social Bias in
   Large Language Models through Self-Reflection
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  When evaluated via explicit self-report, LLMs project neutrality; but
  Implicit Association Tests reveal significant underlying stereotypical
  biases. This exposes a dissociation between explicit and implicit
  measures.

- Methodology:
  Administer both explicit self-report assessments and implicit association
  tests to LLMs; compare explicit vs. implicit bias measures; analyze
  dissociation patterns.

- Relevance to my work:
  Directly relevant dissociation finding: self-reports can mask internal
  biases, analogous to your finding that greedy self-reports can collapse
  while internal state (probe-measured) continues to vary. Underscores the
  need for implicit (probe-based) measurement alongside explicit self-report.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Social bias domain. No emotive concepts. No probing for internal concept
  directions. No multi-turn conversation. No temporal dynamics. No steering.
  No numeric self-report of internal state.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite as a clear example of the explicit-implicit dissociation in model
  self-assessment, directly motivating why your probe-based independent
  measurement is necessary.

8. Wang, Hu, and Wagner (2025) — JULI: Jailbreak Large Language Models by
   Self-Introspection
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  LLMs can be jailbroken by exploiting their self-introspection mechanisms:
  accessing and perturbing token-level log-probability distributions can
  expose suppressed harmful capabilities, proving that introspection is a
  dual-use capability.

- Methodology:
  Train a lightweight BiasNet to perturb token log-probabilities and
  extract suppressed harmful content; black-box and white-box attacks;
  evaluated on jailbreak benchmarks.

- Relevance to my work:
  Shows that token-level introspective access (log-probability distributions)
  contains information beyond surface outputs — the same insight underlying
  your logit-based self-report estimator. Also demonstrates that
  introspective capabilities are safety-relevant.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Adversarial security application. No probing for concept directions. No
  emotive concepts. No multi-turn conversation. No temporal dynamics. No
  self-report tracking of internal state. No constructive use for
  understanding introspection quality.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite as evidence that introspective access has safety implications and
  that token-level probability distributions contain rich information
  beyond surface outputs.

9. Dung and Tagliabue (2025) — Measuring Language Model Welfare Based on
   Verbal Report: An Analogical-Abductive Approach
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Self-reports from LLMs, when validated against clusters of non-verbal
  behaviors, can serve as a methodological proxy for potential welfare states
  without assuming definitive consciousness.

- Methodology:
  Analogical-abductive framework: compare verbal self-reports with non-verbal
  behavioral indicators; assess convergence as evidence for welfare-relevant
  states.

- Relevance to my work:
  Directly parallels your approach: they validate verbal reports against
  non-verbal behavioral measures, while you validate verbal reports against
  probe-defined internal states. Both use convergent evidence rather than
  taking reports at face value.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Uses behavioral correlates, not internal activation probing. No concept
  directions. No steering. No multi-turn conversational tracking. No
  temporal dynamics. No logit-based estimation. No emotive concept probing.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite for the convergent-evidence approach to validating self-reports.
  Your work provides a stronger form of validation by directly measuring
  internal state via probes rather than relying on behavioral proxies.

10. Tagliabue and Dung (2025) — Probing the Preferences of a Language Model:
    Integrating Verbal and Behavioral Tests of AI Welfare
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  LLMs express consistent conversational preferences ("conversational
  attractors") that frequently correlate with their actual exploratory
  behavior in simulated environments.

- Methodology:
  Verbal preference elicitation; behavioral testing in simulated virtual
  environments; correlation analysis between stated preferences and revealed
  behavior.

- Relevance to my work:
  Shows that model self-reports can correlate with behavior, supporting the
  idea that self-reports contain some genuine information. Your probe-based
  validation provides a more direct test.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Behavioral testing in simulated environments, not internal state
  measurement. No probing. No concept directions. No steering. No multi-
  turn conversation with self-report tracking. No temporal dynamics, No
  emotive concept tracking.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite for evidence that self-reports correlate with some form of "ground
  truth" behavior. Your work uses probes as a more rigorous internal
  ground truth.

11. Khanna et al. (2025) — SELF-PERCEPT: Introspection Improves LLM Detection
    of Multi-Person Mental Manipulation in Conversations
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Prompting LLMs to introspectively analyze multi-person conversational
  dynamics significantly improves detection of subtle mental manipulation
  and coercion.

- Methodology:
  SELF-PERCEPT prompting framework: structured introspective analysis of
  conversational behaviors before making judgments; evaluated on multi-
  person manipulation detection benchmarks.

- Relevance to my work:
  Demonstrates a practical application of structured self-reflection in
  conversational contexts. The multi-person conversation setting is related
  to your multi-turn conversational paradigm.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Applied introspection for task performance (manipulation detection), not
  self-report of internal concept state. No probing. No steering. No
  numeric self-reports. No emotive concept tracking. No temporal dynamics.
  No internal state measurement.

- Overall takeaway:
  Minor citation. Shows practical value of conversational introspection
  but in a fundamentally different application.

12. Berg, de Lucena, and Rosenblatt (2025) — Large Language Models Report
    Subjective Experience Under Self-Referential Processing
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Placing models into sustained self-referential processing loops reliably
  elicits structured first-person claims of subjective experience across
  architectures. These claims are mechanistically gated by specific latent
  features in sparse autoencoders, showing a deep link between internal
  representations and self-reports.

- Methodology:
  Self-referential processing prompts; sparse autoencoder analysis of which
  latent features correlate with subjective experience claims; cross-
  architecture comparisons.

- Relevance to my work:
  Demonstrates a mechanistic link between internal representations and
  self-reports — the core question of your paper. The SAE-gating finding
  suggests that self-reports are not arbitrary text but are tied to specific
  internal features.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Subjective experience claims (qualitative), not numeric self-reports.
  Single-turn or loop-based, not naturalistic multi-turn conversation with
  a user. No temporal dynamics. No contrastive concept probes. No steering
  for causal testing. No emotive concept tracking over time. Uses SAEs
  rather than your linear probe + steering framework.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite as evidence that self-reports have mechanistic grounding in internal
  representations. Your work extends this by testing whether graded numeric
  self-reports of specific emotive concepts track independently measured
  internal state across conversation time.

13. Li, Phuong, and Tan (2025) — Spilling the Beans: Teaching LLMs to
    Self-Report Their Hidden Objectives
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  LLMs can be taught to honestly report hidden misaligned objectives through
  "Self-Report Fine-Tuning" (SRFT). Training on factual honesty generalizes:
  models learn to confess adversarial or hidden instructions.

- Methodology:
  Fine-tune models to admit factual errors (SRFT); test generalization to
  reporting hidden adversarial objectives; evaluate honesty transfer.

- Relevance to my work:
  Shows that honest self-reporting is a trainable and generalizable
  capability, paralleling your finding that steering can improve
  introspective reporting. The generalization result is especially relevant
  to your cross-concept findings.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Requires fine-tuning on honesty. Tests hidden objectives (safety-relevant),
  not emotive concept states. No probing. No multi-turn conversation. No
  temporal dynamics. No activation steering. No numeric self-reports of
  internal state.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite for the finding that honest self-reporting generalizes across
  domains, supporting your cross-concept steering results.

14. Han et al. (2025) — The Personality Illusion: Revealing Dissociation
    Between Self-Reports and Behavior in LLMs
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Post-training alignment induces stable, human-like personality traits in
  LLM self-reports, but these stated traits poorly predict actual downstream
  behavior. This reveals an "illusion of coherence" created by alignment.

- Methodology:
  Administer personality questionnaires to LLMs; compare self-reported
  personality traits with actual behavioral patterns in downstream tasks;
  analyze dissociation.

- Relevance to my work:
  Directly motivates your core argument: self-reports need independent
  validation because alignment creates an illusion of coherence. Your probe-
  based framework directly addresses this by providing an independent
  measure of internal state against which to evaluate self-reports.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Personality trait assessment, not emotive state tracking. No probing. No
  steering. No multi-turn conversation. No temporal dynamics. No numeric
  self-report of graded internal concept state. No logit-based estimation.

- Overall takeaway:
  Key citation for motivating your entire framework. If self-reports can
  be "illusory," independent internal measurement is essential — which is
  exactly what your probes provide.
