PAPER-BY-PAPER ANALYSIS — 01 Introspection and Self-Report
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Kadavath et al. (2022) — Language Models (Mostly) Know What They Know
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  LLMs can be trained to evaluate P(True) (probability a proposed answer is
  correct) and P(IK) (probability they can answer a question). Larger models
  are better calibrated about their own knowledge boundaries.

- Methodology:
  Prompted self-evaluation on closed-ended QA tasks; calibration curves
  comparing predicted confidence vs. actual accuracy; scaling analysis across
  model sizes.

- Relevance to my work:
  Establishes the foundational idea that LLMs have some metacognitive access
  to their own competence. My work inherits this premise but applies it to
  emotive internal states rather than factual correctness.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Focuses exclusively on factual correctness and single-shot QA settings, not
  on emotive or affective concept states. No multi-turn conversation analysis.
  No comparison of self-report with independently measured internal
  representations (probes). No temporal dynamics. No causal interventions.

- Overall takeaway:
  Foundational reference for the claim that LLMs possess basic self-knowledge.
  Cite as the starting point from which your work departs by testing richer,
  non-factual, temporally evolving self-reports.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. Yin et al. (2023) — Do LLMs Know What They Don't Know?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  LLMs can identify some questions they cannot answer, but this ability is
  limited and inconsistent. Models often fail to abstain when they should.

- Methodology:
  Probing LLM responses across answerable and unanswerable question sets;
  evaluating refusal and abstention behavior; comparisons across model sizes
  and prompting strategies.

- Relevance to my work:
  Confirms that self-knowledge in LLMs is partial and variable, motivating the
  need for better measurement tools like my probe-report framework.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Restricted to factual knowledge boundaries. Single-turn, benchmark-style
  evaluation. No internal state measurement. No emotive concepts. No temporal
  or conversational structure.

- Overall takeaway:
  Use to argue that even basic metacognitive access is imperfect, reinforcing
  why a rigorous operational framework (like yours) is needed.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Shinn et al. (2023) — Reflexion: Language Agents with Verbal Reinforcement
   Learning
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  LLM agents can learn from prior mistakes by generating verbal self-feedback
  (reflections) stored in episodic memory, improving performance across
  sequential trials without weight updates.

- Methodology:
  Agentic framework with trial-based task execution; verbal reflections stored
  as text; evaluated on decision-making, reasoning, and coding benchmarks.

- Relevance to my work:
  Shows that models can generate meaningful self-evaluative text that guides
  future behavior, a form of functional metacognition.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Reflexion is benchmark-style output optimization, not measurement of
  internal state. No numeric self-reports. No probes. No multi-turn
  conversation with users. No emotive or affective concepts. No causal
  connection to internal representations.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite as an example of "self-reflection for task improvement" paradigm that
  your work fundamentally differs from: you test whether self-reports track
  internal states, not whether they improve task performance.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. Ren et al. (2023) — Self-Evaluation Improves Selective Generation in LLMs
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Token-level self-evaluation allows LLMs to estimate response quality and
  selectively abstain from answering when uncertain, improving reliability.

- Methodology:
  Models generate responses and then self-evaluate using token probabilities;
  selective generation where low-confidence outputs are suppressed.

- Relevance to my work:
  Demonstrates that internal token-level signals carry meaningful information
  about output quality, analogous to my use of logit-based self-report
  estimators.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Purely about factual correctness and selective generation. No emotive
  concepts. No multi-turn interaction. No comparison with probe-defined
  internal state. No temporal evolution of self-assessment.

- Overall takeaway:
  Useful as precedent for using token-level signals for metacognition, but
  clearly distinct from your application to emotive state tracking.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Madaan et al. (2023) — Self-Refine: Iterative Refinement with Self-Feedback
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  LLMs can iteratively improve their outputs by generating natural language
  feedback to themselves and refining accordingly, without supervised training.

- Methodology:
  Generate → self-critique → refine loop; evaluated on code generation,
  reasoning, sentiment, and dialogue tasks.

- Relevance to my work:
  Another demonstration that models can produce self-evaluative information.
  Establishes the broader "self-feedback" paradigm.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  This is output optimization, not introspection about internal state. No
  internal measurements. No numeric self-reports about affect or emotive
  concepts. No probes, no steering, no temporal dynamics, no multi-turn
  naturalistic conversations.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite briefly as part of the "self-correction/refinement" paradigm that
  precedes your more specific question about whether reports actually track
  internal state.

6. Zhang et al. (2024) — Self-Alignment for Factuality: Mitigating
   Hallucinations in LLMs via Self-Evaluation
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Self-evaluation scores can be used as reward signals for factuality
  alignment, reducing hallucinations without external supervision.

- Methodology:
  Self-evaluation generates quality scores; these are used as rewards for
  alignment fine-tuning; evaluated on factuality benchmarks.

- Relevance to my work:
  Shows that models' self-generated numeric signals can serve as training
  signals, connecting to the idea that self-reports carry real information.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Factuality-focused. No emotive concepts. No comparison with independently
  measured internal state. Single-turn evaluations. No naturalistic
  conversation setting.

- Overall takeaway:
  Background reference for self-evaluation as a useful signal; your work asks
  a fundamentally different question about what those signals index.

7. Comsa and Shanahan (2025) — Does It Make Sense to Speak of Introspection
   in Large Language Models?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  True introspection requires a causal connection between an internal state
  and the self-report about that state; mere mimicry of introspective
  language is not sufficient. LLMs may confabulate rather than genuinely
  introspect.

- Methodology:
  Philosophical and conceptual analysis; definition of criteria for genuine
  introspection (causal dependence on internal state, not just pattern
  matching).

- Relevance to my work:
  Directly motivates your operational definition of introspection and your
  causal testing approach. Your work is essentially an empirical response to
  the criteria they articulate.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Purely conceptual, no empirical data. Does not test whether introspection
  actually occurs under any operational definition. No experiments, no probes,
  no steering, no numeric self-reports.

- Overall takeaway:
  Essential conceptual reference. Cite when introducing your operational
  definition of introspection and when justifying why causal tests (steering)
  are necessary. Your paper provides the empirical evidence they call for.

8. Ackerman (2025) — Evidence for Limited Metacognition in LLMs
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  LLM metacognition is limited and qualitatively different from human
  metacognition; models rely on external cues rather than genuine internal
  signals.

- Methodology:
  Review and analysis of existing metacognitive evidence; comparison with
  human metacognitive processes.

- Relevance to my work:
  Supports the view that default LLM self-assessment may be unreliable,
  motivating your finding that greedy reports collapse and that logit-based
  methods are needed to recover meaningful variation.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Does not propose or test an operational measure of metacognition. No
  probing, no steering, no numeric self-report analysis. No multi-turn
  conversation paradigm.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite as motivation for why simple prompting may not reveal true
  metacognitive capacity, and why your richer extraction methods are
  necessary.

9. Chen et al. (2025) — From Imitation to Introspection: Probing
   Self-Consciousness in Language Models
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Uses structural causal games (SCGs) to formally define and probe concepts
  like belief, intention, and self-reflection in LLMs. Finds that models
  weakly represent these concepts but they are difficult to edit.

- Methodology:
  SCG-based framework with linear probes on internal representations;
  counterfactual interventions to test editability of self-consciousness
  concepts.

- Relevance to my work:
  Closely related methodology (probing internal representations for abstract
  concepts). Shows that abstract cognitive concepts can be linearly probed.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Uses philosophical/cognitive concepts (belief, intention) rather than
  emotive states. Single-turn probing, not naturalistic multi-turn
  conversations. No turn-by-turn temporal analysis. No numeric self-reports.
  No direct comparison of self-report with internal state. Focuses on
  editability rather than tracking fidelity over time.

- Overall takeaway:
  Good methodological parallel. Cite to show that probing abstract cognitive
  concepts is established, and contrast by noting that your work focuses on
  emotive states in naturalistic settings with temporal structure.

10. Sun and Zeng (2025) — Introspection of Thought Helps AI Agents
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Multi-agent logical reasoning within a single LLM (Introspection of
  Thought, INoT) outperforms chain-of-thought by 7-8% on classic reasoning
  benchmarks.

- Methodology:
  PromptCode-based framework with virtual debate agents inside a single model;
  evaluated on logical reasoning benchmarks.

- Relevance to my work:
  Demonstrates value of self-reflective reasoning architectures, tangentially
  related to the question of what models know about themselves.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Task-optimization focused (reasoning benchmarks). No emotive concepts. No
  internal state measurement. No probes or steering. No numeric self-reports.
  No multi-turn conversational setting with users.

- Overall takeaway:
  Minor citation. Useful to show that "introspection" is being used broadly
  in the field, while your work uses a specific operational definition tied
  to internal state tracking.

11. Song, Hu, and Mahowald (2025) — Language Models Fail to Introspect About
    Their Knowledge of Language
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Prompted metalinguistic judgments do not tap into privileged self-knowledge;
  LLMs fail to introspect on their own grammatical knowledge. Within-model
  effects vanish when model similarity is controlled.

- Methodology:
  Metalinguistic prompting; comparison of model self-reports with actual
  probability distributions over linguistic structures; cross-model
  comparisons to test for privileged access.

- Relevance to my work:
  Important negative result showing limits of prompted introspection for
  linguistic knowledge. Contrasts with your positive results for emotive
  concepts, possibly because emotive states are represented differently than
  linguistic knowledge.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Tests linguistic/grammatical introspection, not emotive or affective states.
  No probes for internal concept directions. No steering. No multi-turn
  conversation. No temporal dynamics. No logit-based estimators.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite as evidence that introspection is not uniformly available across all
  domains, and to position your finding that emotive states may be more
  accessible to self-report than linguistic knowledge.

12. Binder et al. (2025) — Looking Inward: Language Models Can Learn About
    Themselves by Introspection
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  LLMs possess a degree of "privileged access" — they can predict their own
  hypothetical behaviors better than other models can predict them,
  demonstrating genuine self-knowledge.

- Methodology:
  Self-prediction vs. cross-prediction paradigm; train models to predict
  their own output distributions on hypothetical queries; compare self-
  prediction accuracy to cross-model prediction.

- Relevance to my work:
  Strong precedent showing that LLMs have some form of genuine self-access
  beyond pattern matching. Supports the premise that self-reports may contain
  real information about internal states.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Tests behavioral self-prediction (output distributions), not internal
  emotive state tracking. Single-shot evaluation, not multi-turn. No probes.
  No steering. No numeric self-reports of emotive concepts. No temporal
  dynamics. Uses very large models.

- Overall takeaway:
  Key citation for establishing that privileged self-access exists. Your work
  extends this by testing whether that access extends to tracking emotive
  internal states through time in conversation.

13. Ji-An et al. (2025) — Language Models Are Capable of Metacognitive
    Monitoring and Control of Their Internal Activations
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  LLMs can be taught via in-context learning to report and implicitly control
  specific target axes in their neural activation space, analogous to a
  neurofeedback paradigm.

- Methodology:
  In-context learning with labeled examples of activation-report pairs;
  models learn to report numeric values corresponding to activation
  projections along target directions; also tests implicit control (steering
  activations by intention).

- Relevance to my work:
  Most closely related prior work. Also correlates self-reports with internal
  activation state and tests for control. Uses the term "metacognition"
  which your work relates to. Demonstrates that reporting and control are
  possible in principle.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Constrained paradigm with in-context learning of labeled examples (not
  naturalistic conversation). Models are taught what to report via examples,
  rather than spontaneously reporting. Not emotive concepts but arbitrary
  activation directions. No multi-turn conversation with a simulated user.
  No temporal evolution analysis (turn-by-turn coupling). No greedy vs.
  logit-based comparison of report quality. No cross-concept steering
  interactions. Limited analysis of model scale effects.

- Overall takeaway:
  Essential citation, closest methodological relative. Frame your work as
  extending this by removing the in-context learning scaffold, using
  naturalistic conversations, emotive concepts, temporal analysis, and richer
  report extraction methods. Your paradigm tests introspection "in the wild"
  rather than in a constrained learned task.

14. Plunkett et al. (2025) — Self-Interpretability: LLMs Can Describe Complex
    Internal Processes that Drive Their Decisions
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Fine-tuned models can accurately self-report the internal attribute weights
  driving multi-attribute choices (r ≈ 0.74–0.75 post-training), and this
  capability generalizes to novel contexts.

- Methodology:
  Train models to make decisions based on specific quantitative attribute
  weights; then test whether models can report those weights in natural
  language; measure correlation between actual weights and reported weights.

- Relevance to my work:
  Demonstrates that models can report quantitative internal decision variables,
  which directly parallels your numeric self-report framework. Shows that
  self-report can be numerically precise.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Requires fine-tuning to establish the introspective capability. Tests
  static, trained attribute weights, not dynamic emotive states that emerge
  naturally. Single-shot evaluation. No multi-turn conversation. No
  temporal dynamics. No probe-defined independent internal measurement.
  No causal steering tests. Only very large models (GPT-4-level).

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite for precedent on numeric self-report of internal variables. Contrast
  by noting your work tests naturally emergent (not fine-tuned) emotive
  states in small models across multi-turn conversations.

15. Li and Zhao (2025) — Self-Reflection Enhances LLMs Towards Substantial
    Academic Response
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  A dual-loop reflection framework (extrospection + introspection) improves
  academic response quality by helping models iterate on their outputs.

- Methodology:
  Two-step reflection: extrospection (external feedback integration) and
  introspection (internal self-assessment); evaluated on academic writing
  quality benchmarks.

- Relevance to my work:
  Another example of the self-reflection for improvement paradigm.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Output quality optimization, not internal state measurement. No probes, no
  steering, no numeric self-reports of internal concept states. No multi-turn
  conversational setting. No temporal dynamics.

- Overall takeaway:
  Minor citation in the "self-refinement" family. Clearly distinct from your
  internal-state-tracking paradigm.

16. Jackson et al. (2025) — Simulated Self-Assessment in LLMs: A Psychometric
    Approach to AI Self-Efficacy
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  LLM self-assessments on the General Self-Efficacy Scale (GSES) are highly
  stable but do not reliably reflect actual task abilities. Self-reports
  appear to be learned communication postures rather than genuine assessments.

- Methodology:
  Administer standardized psychometric questionnaires (GSES) to LLMs; compare
  self-reported self-efficacy with actual task performance across domains.

- Relevance to my work:
  Shows that model self-reports can be decoupled from actual capabilities,
  motivating the need for independent validation of self-reports against
  internal measurements, exactly what your probe-based framework provides.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Uses standardized questionnaires (not probes). No internal state measurement.
  No emotive concepts. No multi-turn conversation. No temporal dynamics. No
  causal interventions. Treats self-report as a static trait.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite as evidence that self-reports need independent validation. Your probe-
  based approach directly addresses this limitation.

17. Prestes (2025) — Simulated Selfhood in LLMs: A Behavioral Analysis of
    Introspective Coherence
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  LLMs can mimic introspection but suffer from "diachronic continuity"
  failures — they cannot sustain a consistent self-representation across
  repeated, independent prompts over time.

- Methodology:
  Repeated introspective prompting over multiple sessions; analysis of
  consistency and coherence of self-representations across time.

- Relevance to my work:
  Identifies the temporal consistency problem that is central to your work.
  Your turn-by-turn analysis within conversations is precisely the kind of
  temporal measurement this paper calls for.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Tests consistency of self-identity claims, not tracking of internal emotive
  states. Independent prompts (not multi-turn conversations). No probes, no
  steering. No comparison of self-report with independently measured internal
  state. No numeric self-reports. No causal tests.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite when motivating the temporal dimension of your work. Your paper
  directly addresses the diachronic limitations identified here by measuring
  probe-report coupling turn by turn within conversations.

18. Rivera (2025) — Training Introspective Behavior: Fine-Tuning Induces
    Reliable Internal State Detection in a 7B Model
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  The ability to detect transient, single-token injected concepts can be
  explicitly induced into 7B models via fine-tuning, turning a rare emergent
  trait into a reliable capability.

- Methodology:
  Fine-tune a 7B model to detect concept vector injections in its activations;
  evaluate detection accuracy pre and post fine-tuning.

- Relevance to my work:
  Shows that introspective detection of internal state changes can be trained
  into small models. Related to the idea that introspective capacity can be
  modulated, paralleling your finding that steering can improve introspection.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Detection of injected concepts (a binary recognition task), not graded
  numeric reporting of natural emotive states. Requires fine-tuning. Single-
  turn injection detection. No multi-turn conversation. No temporal dynamics.
  No comparison of self-report with probe-defined internal state through
  time. No naturalistic setting.

- Overall takeaway:
  Cite as evidence that introspective capacity is malleable in small models.
  Contrast by noting your work studies naturally occurring (not injected)
  states without fine-tuning, in graded numeric form, across conversation time.

19. Lindsey (2026) — Emergent Introspective Awareness in Large Language Models
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Central finding:
  Large models (Claude Opus 4/4.1) can detect concept vector injections ~20% of
  the time with near-zero false positives. They can distinguish internally
  forced outputs from intended ones and update their self-reports accordingly.

- Methodology:
  Inject concept activation vectors into model forward passes; test whether
  models can identify, name, and distinguish the injected concept; measure
  detection rates, false positive rates, and apology behavior.

- Relevance to my work:
  Establishes causal link between internal state changes and self-reports.
  Most directly relevant precedent for the claim that models can notice
  changes in their internal state. The concept injection paradigm is related
  to your steering approach.

- What it lacks relative to my work:
  Tests detection of externally injected concepts, which is conceptually
  "detecting a special event" rather than continuous tracking of natural
  internal state. Only very large models (Claude Opus 4). Single-shot,
  not multi-turn conversational. No graded numeric self-reports. No turn-by-
  turn temporal dynamics. Not emotive concepts but injected activations.
  Very low detection rates (20%).

- Overall takeaway:
  Key citation for the causal connection between internal states and self-
  reports. Your work extends this from "detecting a special injected event"
  to "continuous numeric measurement of natural internal state over time"
  in much smaller models and naturalistic settings.
