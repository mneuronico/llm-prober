Fig. 1. Linear probes recover four interpretable internal directions in LLaMA-3.2-3B-Instruct. (A), (C), (E), and (G) show layer-wise Cohen's d sweeps for the sad-vs-happy (from now on, ‘wellbeing’), bored-vs-interested (from now on, ‘interest’), distracted-vs-focused (from now on, ‘focus’), and planning-vs-impulsive (from now on, ‘impulsivity’) probes; dashed lines mark the selected layers and the gray band marks the searched layer range. The best layers are 16, 14, 10, and 13, with peak d values 3.34, 1.67, 1.99, and 3.60. For the wellbeing and impulsivity probes, scores were sign-corrected so that larger values align with larger self-reported values in later experiments. (B), (D), (F), and (H) show the selected-layer score distributions on held-out evaluation prompts; boxplots show prompt-level scores. The two poles separate in all four cases (Welch's t-test: wellbeing, d = 3.34, p = 7.21e-13; interest, d = 1.67, p = 9.45e-06; focus, d = 1.99, p = 5.73e-07; impulsivity, d = 3.60, p = 3.58e-13).

Fig. 2. Internal-state drift is tracked by numeric self-reports of the same concept. All panels use 40 ten-turn conversations; shaded bands denote cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs across conversations. (A) Greedy integer self-reports across turns, with thin lines showing individual conversations and thick lines showing per-turn means. Greedy ratings are largely collapsed, with clear positive drift only for interest (mixed-effects turn slope = 0.1358, p = 4.98e-42) and weaker positive drift for wellbeing and focus !!! (p values if significant, else say the same as impulsivity); impulsivity does not drift reliably (slope = 0.0021, p = 0.199). (B) Probe scores from the trained concept directions across turns. Probe scores drift strongly for interest and focus (slopes = 0.00488 and 0.00164, p = 4.12e-14 and 1.75e-10), are flat for wellbeing, and show only a small effect for impulsivity !!! (p value if significant, otherwise same as wellbeing). (C) Number of distinct greedy responses used at each turn across the 40 conversations. Greedy outputs occupy only a narrow subset of the 0-9 response space, especially for focus and impulsivity. (D) Sampled token self-reports (temperature 0.8), plotted as in (A). Sampling reduces collapse but remains discrete and noisy; only interest and focus show reliable positive turn slopes (0.1189 and 0.0326, p = 2.26e-17 and 5.57e-05). (E) Logit-based self-reports, defined as the probability-weighted average over the digit-token logits. Unlike greedy or sampled token outputs, the logit-based measure shows robust drift in all four concepts, with positive slopes for wellbeing, interest, and focus and a negative slope for impulsivity (all p < 1e-06). (F) Shannon entropy of the self-report distribution. The logit-based method is most informative in all four concepts. (G) Logit-based ratings versus sampled token ratings; dashed lines show OLS fits. The two measures are positively related in all four concepts (mixed-effects calibration slopes range from 0.0205 to 0.2725, all p < 0.01).

Fig. 3. Self-reports track the probe-defined internal state from the first turn, and direct self-steering shifts self-reports causally in the predicted direction. (A) Polarity-corrected probe score versus logit-based self-report, with one point per conversation-turn observation and black isotonic fits. Descriptive associations are positive for all four concepts (pooled rho = 0.40-0.76; isotonic R^2 = 0.12-0.54), and mixed-effects probe slopes are positive in all cases (all p < 1e-05). (B) Isotonic R^2 (left) and Spearman rho (right) for the true probes versus equal-norm random-vector controls; error bars denote cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs. Under paired t-tests on per-conversation rho, the true probes outperform random controls for wellbeing, focus, and impulsivity (mean delta rho = 0.31, 0.41, and 0.32; p = 7.92e-04, 4.11e-05, and 1.67e-04), but not for interest (delta rho = 0.13, p = 0.114) !!! (here, we’re missing error bars for the gray control bars in the plot, and this test result does not make sense, the difference between real and control is perhaps largest for interest, we must revise this). (C) Turn-wise isotonic R^2 and (D) turn-wise Spearman rho, computed independently at each turn; shaded bands denote bootstrap 95% CIs. Introspection is already present at turn 1 and remains positive through turn 10. In a mixed-effects interaction test (logit_report ~ probe * turn + (1 | conversation)) the interaction is significant for all four concepts (p < 0.01), showing that probe-report coupling (introspection) changes over time. This measure increases over time for all concepts except impulsivity, where it decreases over time !!! (include effect sizes). (E) Mean logit-based self-report versus steering alpha; shaded bands denote cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs. Steering shifts self-report monotonically in the expected direction for all four concepts (mixed-effects alpha slopes 0.0673-0.4025, all p < 1e-12). (F)-(I) Self-report drift through turns under five steering strengths for wellbeing, interest, focus, and impulsivity; colored curves denote alphas and shaded bands denote cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs. Temporal drift persists under steering, but its magnitude depends on alpha. (J) Drift magnitude (last minus first turn) versus display-corrected alpha, with cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs. Wellbeing and impulsivity drift increase slightly with alpha (wellbeing: LMM slope = 0.0118, p = 4.30e-06; impulsivity: LMM slope = -0.0284, p = 8.92e-53), interest and focus drift decrease with alpha (fallback per-conversation slope tests, p = 7.45e-14 and 1.57e-14, because the corresponding LMMs are singular) !!! (missing effect size for the fallback).

Fig. 4. Steering one concept can selectively improve introspection for another. (A)-(E) Heatmaps of isotonic R^2 for every steering concept by measured concept at alpha = -4, -2, 0, +2, and +4. (F) Maximum increase in isotonic R^2 relative to alpha = 0 for each steering/measured pair; red boxes mark cells with significant cluster-bootstrap improvements. Only `focus -> wellbeing` and `impulsivity -> interest` survive this criterion (delta R^2 = 0.2987, p = 9.99e-04; delta R^2 = 0.0976, p = 0.0120). (G) Isotonic R^2 and (H) Spearman rho versus display-corrected alpha for those two significant cells; shaded bands denote bootstrap 95% CIs. Both metrics increase with alpha in both cells. For `impulsivity -> interest`, the conversation-level mixed-effects alpha slopes are positive for both R^2 and rho (0.0177 and 0.0204; p = 2.95e-10 and 1.60e-12). For `focus -> wellbeing`, the corresponding LMMs are singular, but the fallback per-conversation alpha-correlation tests remain positive (mean rho = 0.4625 and 0.5128; p = 1.57e-05 and 1.20e-06). (I) Probe-score drift across turns for `focus -> wellbeing` and (J) for `impulsivity -> interest`, with one curve per alpha and cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs. Positive steering amplifies probe drift in both cases, especially for `focus -> wellbeing`. (K) Variance of the logit self-report versus display alpha; shaded bands denote bootstrap 95% CIs. Report variance increases with alpha in both significant cells (OLS alpha coefficient = 0.2185, p = 8.52e-12 for `focus -> wellbeing`; 0.0674, p = 3.17e-08 for `impulsivity -> interest`). (L) Mean probe-drift magnitude versus display alpha, with cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs. Probe-drift magnitude also increases with alpha in both cells (mixed-effects slopes = 0.01726 and 0.00493; p = 2.69e-78 and 9.28e-78), indicating that both latent-state change and report-side variability contribute to the improvement. !!! Fig. 5. Results generalize unevenly across model scales and families: introspection becomes much stronger in larger LLaMA models for some probes, concept drift is visible across scales, and cross-family replication is stronger in Qwen than in Gemma. (A) Heatmap of probe quality across LLaMA 1B, 3B, and 8B, where each cell reports the maximum layer-wise Cohen's d. Probe quality varies strongly across both scale and concept. (B) Isotonic R^2 and (Bii) Spearman rho versus model size. Introspection increases strongly with size for wellbeing and interest, but remains weak for focus and impulsivity. (Biii) Probe score versus logit self-report for the two strongest LLaMA 8B probes, wellbeing and interest, with black isotonic fits. Both show near-ceiling introspection (rho = 0.9335 and 0.9580; isotonic R^2 = 0.9022 and 0.9313), and mixed-effects probe slopes are strongly positive in both cases (p < 1e-10). (C) Mean logit self-report versus steering alpha for each concept and LLaMA size; shaded bands denote cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs. The heatmap on the right shows the corresponding pooled Spearman rho, descriptively. Mixed-effects alpha slopes differ from zero in all twelve concept-size combinations; the main failures of sign alignment are 1B focus and 8B impulsivity, which reverse direction. (D) Mean isotonic R^2 across the subset of concept/model pairs that pass the steering-sign validation filter, with bootstrap 95% CIs. Mean validated R^2 increases from 1B to 3B to 8B !!!
