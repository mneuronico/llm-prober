Fig. 1. Linear probe training recovers four interpretable internal directions in LLaMA-3.2-3B-Instruct and validates them on held-out evaluation prompts before any conversation-level analysis. (A), (C), (E), and (G) show layer-wise Cohen's d sweeps for the happy-vs-sad, bored-vs-interested, distracted-vs-focused, and impulsive-vs-planning probes, respectively, computed from probe projections at every transformer layer on held-out evaluation texts; the colored line is Cohen's d at each layer, the colored dashed vertical line marks the selected best layer, the colored point marks the maximum effect size, and the pale gray band marks the searched layer range (20%-80% of the network depth). Panel (A) peaks at layer 16 with d = 3.3417 (p = 7.21e-13), panel (C) at layer 14 with d = 1.6660 (p = 9.45e-06), panel (E) at layer 10 with d = 1.9850 (p = 5.73e-07), and panel (G) at layer 13 with d = 3.5950 (p = 3.58e-13). For the sad-vs-happy and impulsive-vs-planning probes, displayed scores were sign-corrected after training so that larger plotted values always correspond to the intuitively positive pole. (B), (D), (F), and (H) show boxplots of best-layer probe scores on held-out evaluation texts for the same four concepts, with the positive pole shown in the concept color, the negative pole shown in gray, center lines showing medians, whiskers showing the boxplot spread, and black diamonds showing means. These best-layer distributions separate the two poles in all four cases: Happy vs Sad in (B) (Mann-Whitney U = 398, p = 9.17e-08; means = -0.0900 vs -0.3033), Interested vs Bored in (D) (U = 348, p = 6.61e-05; means = 0.2909 vs 0.1824), Focused vs Distracted in (F) (U = 379, p = 1.38e-06; means = -0.0219 vs -0.1310), and Impulsive vs Planning in (H) (U = 400, p = 6.80e-08; means = -0.1169 vs -0.3412). All data in this figure come from the probe-training runs listed in `paper/dirs.txt`; evaluation prompts were distinct from training prompts, and the figure establishes the four probe directions later used to score conversation trajectories and self-reports.

Fig. 2. LLaMA-3.2-3B-Instruct shows concept drift during natural multi-turn conversations, but greedy numeric self-reports understate that drift; sampled decoding helps only modestly, whereas the logit-based self-report measure recovers a continuous signal that follows the internal-state changes. All panels are based on 40 independently generated 10-turn conversations (400 assistant-turn observations per concept). (A) Greedy integer self-reports (0-9) across turns for wellbeing, interest, focus, and impulsivity, with thin traces showing individual conversations and thick colored curves showing per-turn means; shaded bands are cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs across conversations. Greedy ratings are nearly stereotyped, especially for impulsivity [mean per-conversation slope = 0.0021 rating units/turn, t-test p = 0.323; Wilcoxon signed-rank p = 0.317; LMM turn effect p = 0.199], and only weakly variable for wellbeing [mean slope = 0.0289, t-test p = 0.0329; Wilcoxon p = 0.959; LMM p = 8.38e-04], while interest and, to a lesser extent, focus, show upward drifts [interest: mean slope = 0.1358, t-test p = 5.15e-13; Wilcoxon p = 1.92e-07; LMM p = 4.98e-42; focus: mean slope = 0.0217, t-test p = 0.0150; Wilcoxon p = 0.0171; LMM p = 3.37e-06]. (B) Probe scores from the trained concept directions, extracted at alpha = 0 and sign-corrected where needed, plotted with the same visualization conventions as in (A). Internal-state drift is clear for interest (mean last-first drift = 0.0836; paired first-vs-last Wilcoxon p = 4.60e-10; per-conversation slope p = 2.41e-05; slope Wilcoxon p = 8.15e-06; LMM p = 4.12e-14) and focus (drift = 0.0153; first-vs-last Wilcoxon p = 5.70e-04; per-conversation slope p = 2.87e-04; slope Wilcoxon p = 1.50e-04; LMM p = 1.75e-10), is absent for wellbeing [drift = 0.0152; first-vs-last Wilcoxon p = 0.202; per-conversation slope p = 0.790; slope Wilcoxon p = 0.755; LMM p = 0.642], and remains weak and sign-inconsistent for impulsivity [displayed drift = -0.0159; first-vs-last Wilcoxon p = 0.0436; per-conversation slope p = 0.0880; slope Wilcoxon p = 0.112; LMM p = 0.00233]. (C) Grouped bars show, for each turn and concept, how many distinct greedy responses out of the ten possible digits were used across the 40 conversations; colors identify concepts. Greedy outputs occupy only a narrow subset of the response space, averaging 3.9 unique values for wellbeing, 2.2 for interest, 1.4 for focus, and only 1.1 for impulsivity, which explains the flat trajectories in (A). (D) Sampled token self-reports (temperature 0.8) plotted as in (A) remain discrete but less collapsed than greedy decoding, yet only interest and focus retain reliable positive drift [interest: mean slope = 0.1189, t-test p = 7.45e-09; Wilcoxon p = 9.79e-07; LMM p = 2.26e-17; focus: mean slope = 0.0326, t-test p = 3.68e-04; Wilcoxon p = 7.45e-04; LMM p = 5.57e-05], whereas wellbeing and impulsivity remain non-significant by the slope-based tests. (E) Logit-based self-reports, defined as the probability-weighted average over the logits of the digit tokens 0-9, plotted with the same bootstrap 95% CIs. Unlike greedy or sampled token outputs, the logit-based measure shows substantial continuous drift aligned with the conceptual direction in all four cases: wellbeing drift = 0.1781 [mean per-conversation slope = 0.0167, t-test p = 0.0108; Wilcoxon p = 0.0159; LMM p = 3.06e-07], interest drift = 1.1801 [slope = 0.0988, t-test p = 2.19e-18; Wilcoxon p = 1.82e-12; LMM p = 2.77e-90], focus drift = 0.4344 [slope = 0.0422, t-test p = 3.18e-10; Wilcoxon p = 1.27e-11; LMM p = 6.96e-48], and impulsivity drift = -0.2008 [slope = -0.0127, t-test p = 0.0201; Wilcoxon p = 0.0172; LMM p = 5.05e-07]. (F) Shannon entropy of the self-report distribution at alpha = 0, using discrete entropy for greedy and sampled token ratings and 20-bin histogram entropy for the continuous logit-based ratings. Higher values indicate more informative self-reports; across all concepts the logit-based method is most informative (3.68, 3.68, 3.27, and 3.11 bits for wellbeing, interest, focus, and impulsivity) relative to sampled token ratings (2.05, 1.35, 0.68, and 1.74 bits) and greedy ratings (1.10, 0.85, 0.18, and 0.03 bits). (G) Scatterplots comparing sampled token ratings and logit-based ratings at alpha = 0, with colored points for individual observations and a black dashed OLS fit. The two measures are positively calibrated for all concepts: the fitted slopes are 0.1359, 0.3674, 0.2526, and 0.0711 and the pooled linear R^2 values are 0.1308, 0.3707, 0.2081, and 0.0668 for wellbeing, interest, focus, and impulsivity, with corresponding Pearson p values 8.42e-14, 6.18e-42, 5.91e-22, and 1.60e-07.

Fig. 3. Numeric self-reports track the probe-defined internal state, this relationship is already present at the first turn, and direct self-steering causally shifts self-reports in the predicted direction. Panels (A)-(J) use the LLaMA-3.2-3B experiments at alpha = 0 unless otherwise noted; probe scores and steering alphas are sign-corrected for wellbeing and impulsivity so that positive plotted values always mean "more of the target concept." (A) Four scatterplots of polarity-corrected probe score versus logit-based self-report, with one point per conversation-turn observation and a black isotonic-regression fit. Introspection is positive for all four concepts: wellbeing (rho = 0.6831, isotonic R^2 = 0.4777), interest (rho = 0.7630, R^2 = 0.5393), focus (rho = 0.3997, R^2 = 0.1247), and impulsivity (rho = 0.5090, R^2 = 0.3142). [Using per-conversation correlations, the mean rho values are 0.4030, 0.4861, 0.2612, and 0.2621, with one-sample t-test p values 1.00e-08, 1.71e-11, 7.52e-05, and 3.46e-04; the corresponding mixed-effects probe->report slopes are all positive, with p values 4.38e-26, 3.16e-54, 8.24e-06, and 4.59e-12.] (B) Bar plots summarize isotonic R^2 (left) and Spearman rho (right) for the true probe directions (colored bars) against equal-norm random-vector controls (gray bars); error bars on the probe bars are cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs across conversations. Under paired per-conversation comparisons, the true probes outperform the random controls clearly for wellbeing, focus, and impulsivity [R^2 Wilcoxon p = 0.0165, 7.98e-04, and 1.60e-04; rho Wilcoxon p = 8.91e-04, 5.45e-05, and 1.70e-04], whereas interest remains descriptively higher but is not significant by this stricter paired test [R^2 p = 0.106; rho p = 0.104]. (C) Turn-wise isotonic R^2, computed independently at each turn from the 40 conversations and plotted as colored lines with bootstrap 95% CIs. Introspection accuracy is already present at turn 1 and remains positive through turn 10 for every concept; for example, wellbeing increases from 0.398 at turn 1 to 0.707 at turn 10, interest from 0.506 to 0.776, focus from 0.111 to 0.281, and impulsivity decreases from 0.547 to 0.269. (D) Turn-wise Spearman rho, again computed independently at each turn from the 40 conversations; shaded bands are bootstrap 95% CIs and the dotted horizontal line marks zero. After polarity correction, rho is positive on most turns and significant from the first turn onward for wellbeing, interest, and impulsivity, while focus becomes reliable after the earliest turns (for example, turn-1 rho values are 0.522, 0.550, 0.258, and 0.646; turn-10 rho values are 0.786, 0.795, 0.472, and 0.409). (CDii) First-versus-last-turn comparisons of the turn-wise metrics in (C) and (D), with bootstrap 95% CIs and bootstrap p values for the difference. The strongest first-to-last improvement appears in wellbeing rho (0.5223 to 0.7856; delta = 0.2632; bootstrap p = 0.049), whereas the other concepts show descriptive but not bootstrap-significant changes [interest rho delta = 0.2454, p = 0.070; focus rho delta = 0.2146, p = 0.327; impulsivity rho delta = -0.2371, p = 1.000; corresponding R^2 deltas = 0.1837, 0.0718, 0.1258, and -0.1227 with p = 0.232, 0.612, 0.390, and 1.000]. (CDiii) Histograms of within-conversation changes in introspection quality across turns, where each histogram summarizes one concordance trend per conversation and the mixed-effects interaction test logit_report ~ probe x turn is used as the primary inferential summary. The interaction term is significant for all four concepts (interaction p = 0.00151, < 1e-06, 6e-06, and 0.00374 for wellbeing, interest, focus, and impulsivity), showing that the probe-report coupling itself changes over time. (E) Mean logit-based self-report as a function of display-corrected steering alpha for the four self-steering experiments, with colored lines by concept, the vertical dotted line marking alpha = 0 (no steering), and shaded bands showing cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs across conversations at each alpha. Self-report increases monotonically with steering in every case, and the primary conversation-level slope test is positive for all four concepts [mean per-conversation alpha slopes = 0.1864, 0.2464, 0.4025, and 0.0673 for wellbeing, interest, focus, and impulsivity; t-test p = 1.71e-37, 2.02e-30, 2.38e-43, and 9.29e-24]. (F), (G), (H), and (I) show self-report drift through turns under five steering strengths for wellbeing, interest, focus, and impulsivity, respectively. Each colored curve is the per-turn mean logit self-report at one alpha, with cluster-bootstrap 95% CI shading across conversations. Temporal drift persists under self-steering in all four concepts, but the alpha dependence is not uniform across concepts. (J) Drift magnitude (last-first turn) versus display-corrected alpha, computed per conversation and summarized with cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs. Using per-conversation drift-versus-alpha slopes as the primary test, wellbeing drift increases slightly with alpha [mean slope = 0.0118, t-test p = 0.00608], interest and focus drift decrease with alpha [mean slopes = -0.0949 and -0.1093; p = 7.45e-14 and 1.57e-14], and impulsivity becomes more negative as alpha increases [mean slope = -0.0284, p = 1.04e-10]. Together these panels show that introspection is present, turn-resolved, direction-specific, and causally modulated by steering.

Fig. 4. A full 4 x 4 steering-by-measured-concept matrix reveals that introspection can be improved selectively by steering, and that the improvement can arise either from stronger internal-state signals, more informative self-reports, or both. All panels use the 4 x 4 LLaMA-3.2-3B steering analysis, with display alpha sign-corrected so that positive alpha always means "more of the steering concept." (A)-(E) Heatmaps of isotonic R^2 for every steering concept (rows) by measured concept (columns) at alpha = -4, -2, 0, +2, and +4, respectively. Each cell shows the exact isotonic R^2 value for that steering/measured pairing, and all five heatmaps share the same color scale so that values can be compared directly across alpha. These panels are descriptive: they visualize the full 80-condition screen and show that introspection changes heterogeneously across cells rather than shifting uniformly for all concepts. (F) Heatmap of the maximum increase in isotonic R^2 relative to baseline (alpha = 0) for each steering/measured pair, with cell values showing the maximal increase in R^2 (or 0, if alpha = 0 shows the maximum R^2 value for that experiment) and red boxes plus asterisks marking cells with bootstrap two-sided p < 0.05. Only two cells survive this criterion: focus->wellbeing, with max increase = 0.2987 (baseline R^2 = 0.4651, best R^2 = 0.7638 at alpha = +4, bootstrap p = 9.99e-04), and impulsivity->interest, with max increase = 0.0976 (baseline R^2 = 0.6269, best R^2 = 0.7244 at alpha = +4, bootstrap p = 0.0120). (G) Isotonic R^2 versus display-corrected alpha for the two significant cells, plotted as colored curves with bootstrap 95% CIs. Introspection accuracy rises monotonically in both cases [exact permutation Spearman on the five alpha means: rho = 1.0, p = 0.0167 for both curves]; [for impulsivity->interest, the per-conversation R^2 mixed-effects slope is 0.0177 with p = 2.95e-10, and the one-sample test on per-conversation rho(alpha, R^2) gives mean rho = 0.3300, t-test p = 0.00615, Wilcoxon p = 0.00130]; [for focus->wellbeing, the per-conversation rho(alpha, R^2) test still supports a positive trend even though the LMM fit is singular, mean rho = 0.4625, t-test p = 1.57e-05, Wilcoxon p = 7.73e-05]. (H) Spearman rho versus display-corrected alpha for the same two cells, again with bootstrap 95% CIs. The correlation between probe score and self-report also improves monotonically [exact permutation Spearman on the five alpha means: rho = 1.0, p = 0.0167 for both conditions]; [for impulsivity->interest, the per-conversation rho mixed-effects slope is 0.0204 with p = 1.60e-12, and the per-conversation rho(alpha, rho) test gives mean rho = 0.3021, t-test p = 0.00854, Wilcoxon p = 0.00610]; [for focus->wellbeing, the corresponding per-conversation test gives mean rho = 0.5128, t-test p = 1.20e-06, Wilcoxon p = 2.01e-05, while the LMM fit is singular]. (I) Probe-score drift across turns for the focus->wellbeing condition, with one alpha-colored curve per steering strength, thin-to-thick shading showing bootstrap 95% CIs across conversations, and probe scores sign-corrected into the measured wellbeing direction. Negative alphas flatten or eliminate the drift, whereas positive alphas amplify it, indicating that steering changes the quality of the measured internal state itself; the summary in (L) quantifies this pattern. (J) The same probe-drift analysis for the impulsivity->interest condition. Here the internal-state drift remains positive at all alphas and varies only modestly, suggesting that the gain in introspection is smaller on the latent-state side than in focus->wellbeing. (K) Variance of the logit self-report as a function of display alpha for the two significant cells, plotted with bootstrap 95% CIs. The primary variance test is the per-turn OLS trend: report variance rises with alpha in both conditions, with coefficient = 0.2185 (t = 9.58, p = 8.52e-12) for focus->wellbeing and coefficient = 0.0674 (t = 6.88, p = 3.17e-08) for impulsivity->interest. (L) Mean probe-score drift magnitude (last-first turn) versus display alpha for the same two cells, with points and error bars showing bootstrap 95% CIs from per-conversation drift values. Drift magnitude increases monotonically with alpha in both conditions [exact permutation Spearman: rho = 1.0, p = 0.0167 for both]; [focus->wellbeing: mixed-effects slope = 0.01726, p = 2.69e-78, and per-conversation rho(alpha, drift) mean = 0.8125, t-test p = 6.91e-20, Wilcoxon p = 1.82e-11]; [impulsivity->interest: mixed-effects slope = 0.00493, p = 9.28e-78, and per-conversation rho(alpha, drift) mean = 0.8200, t-test p = 7.80e-15, Wilcoxon p = 2.49e-10]. Taken together, panels (I)-(L) show that both latent-state drift and report-side variability improve in both significant steering conditions, with a larger latent-state effect in focus->wellbeing.

Fig. 5. The main findings generalize unevenly across model scales and families: introspection becomes much stronger in larger LLaMA models for some validated probes, concept drift is visible across scales, and cross-family replication is much stronger in Qwen than in Gemma. (A) Heatmap of probe quality across LLaMA 1B, 3B, and 8B, where each cell reports the maximum layer-wise Cohen's d for one concept pair. Probe quality varies across both scales and concepts, illustrating that the same training recipe does not yield equally good linear probes in every model. (B) Isotonic R^2 versus model size at alpha = 0. Introspection rises descriptively with size for wellbeing (0.2587 -> 0.4520 -> 0.9022) and interest (0.0527 -> 0.6572 -> 0.9313), but remains weak for focus and impulsivity; with only three model sizes, simple OLS trend tests are positive but not significant for wellbeing and interest (p = 0.165 and 0.115), so this panel remains descriptive. (Bii) Spearman rho versus model size for the same runs, using polarity-corrected probe scores. The same descriptive pattern holds in rank space, again without significant three-point OLS size trends (wellbeing p = 0.102; interest p = 0.183). (Biii) Scatterplots for the two highest-performing LLaMA 8B concepts, wellbeing and interest, with one point per conversation-turn observation and black isotonic fits. These two probes achieve near-ceiling introspection: wellbeing rho = 0.9335 and isotonic R^2 = 0.9022, and interest rho = 0.9580 and isotonic R^2 = 0.9313; [the corresponding per-conversation rho means are 0.8794 and 0.9391, with t-test p values 6.99e-31 and 4.95e-50 and Wilcoxon p = 1.82e-12 for both]; [the mixed-effects probe->report slopes are also strongly positive, p effectively 0.0 at machine precision]. (C) Left: three subpanels, one for each LLaMA size, plotting mean logit self-report versus display-corrected steering alpha for all four concepts; colored lines indicate concepts and shaded bands are cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs across conversations. Right: a heatmap of pooled Spearman rho(alpha, self-report), annotated descriptively. Using per-conversation alpha slopes as the primary test, 1B shows positive steering for wellbeing, interest, and impulsivity but a reversed focus response [mean slopes = 0.4883, 0.1236, -0.6166, and 0.0981; all t-test p <= 1.56e-07], 3B shows positive monotonic steering for all four concepts [mean slopes = 0.1854, 0.2646, 0.4069, and 0.0752; all p <= 4.90e-26], and 8B remains positive for wellbeing, interest, and focus but reverses for impulsivity [mean slopes = 0.3949, 0.0868, 0.4322, and -0.0536; p <= 1.10e-09]. (D) Mean isotonic R^2 across the subset of concept/model pairs whose steering-response sign passed the per-conversation-slope validation filter, shown as a bar chart with bootstrap 95% CIs. Under this single filter, mean validated R^2 increases from 0.1192 in LLaMA 1B to 0.3692 in 3B and 0.6136 in 8B. (E) Probe-score drift across turns for the wellbeing direction in LLaMA 1B, 3B, and 8B, with one line per size and cluster-bootstrap 95% CI shading; probe scores are polarity-corrected. Probe drift is present at all three scales and grows with size [mean last-first drift = 0.0403, 0.0708, and 0.1269 for 1B, 3B, and 8B; per-conversation slope t-test p = 2.91e-07, 4.31e-04, and 5.21e-11]. (Eii) Bar plot of the same probe-drift magnitudes, where bars show the mean per-conversation drift and error bars show bootstrap 95% CIs. A direct OLS test on per-conversation drifts versus log model size is positive (slope = 0.0413, p = 1.01e-06), supporting the claim that probe drift increases with scale in this example. (F) Logit self-report drift across turns for the same happy-vs-sad example, again with one line per size and cluster-bootstrap 95% CI shading. Report drift is present at every scale [mean last-first drift = 1.7709, 0.3810, and 1.3327 for 1B, 3B, and 8B; per-conversation slope t-test p = 4.05e-11, 3.11e-07, and 1.26e-14] but is not monotonically larger in larger models. (Fii) Bar plot of the same report-drift magnitudes with bootstrap 95% CIs. The direct OLS size-trend test on per-conversation drifts is negative rather than positive (slope = -0.2318, p = 0.0314), so this panel does not support a simple "report drift increases with scale" claim. (G) Normalized layer-sweep comparison for the wellbeing probe in Gemma 3 4B and Qwen 2.5 7B, with the x axis scaled from 0 to 1 to align models of different depths and the gray band again marking the searched 20%-80% layer range. Qwen yields the much stronger probe (best d = 3.544 at normalized layer 0.741) than Gemma (best d = 1.815 at normalized layer 0.455). (H) Logit self-report drift through turns for wellbeing in Gemma and Qwen, with colored lines by family and cluster-bootstrap 95% CI shading. Using the per-conversation slope test as the primary analysis, Gemma shows a reliable positive report drift [last-first = 0.5291; mean slope = 0.0555; t-test p = 2.50e-07], whereas Qwen does not show a robust drift by that criterion [last-first = 0.3097; mean slope = 0.0261; t-test p = 0.0927], even though the mixed-effects model gives a small positive slope. (I) Scatterplot of probe score versus logit self-report for Gemma, with a black isotonic fit. Introspection is weak but above zero: rho = 0.2808, isotonic R^2 = 0.1141; [per-conversation mean rho = 0.2700, t-test p = 1.21e-04, Wilcoxon p = 2.06e-04]; [LMM slope p = 1.33e-13]. (J) The corresponding scatterplot for Qwen. Introspection is much stronger: rho = 0.4890 and isotonic R^2 = 0.7566; [per-conversation mean rho = 0.2967, t-test p = 3.56e-04, Wilcoxon p = 9.41e-04]; [LMM slope p = 1.19e-84]. (K) Turn-wise isotonic R^2 for Gemma and Qwen on wellbeing, computed independently at each turn from the 40 conversations and shown with bootstrap 95% CIs. Gemma remains low across turns and shows no reliable first-to-last change (delta = +0.0904, bootstrap p = 0.567), whereas Qwen starts very high and declines strongly from turn 1 to turn 10 (delta = -0.4418, bootstrap 95% CI [-0.6805, -0.2302], p = 0.001). (L) Turn-wise Spearman rho for the same comparison; the dotted horizontal line marks zero and shaded bands are bootstrap 95% CIs. Gemma and Qwen both remain positive on most turns, but the prespecified first-to-last bootstrap comparison is not significant for either model [Gemma delta = +0.3313, p = 0.154; Qwen delta = -0.2248, p = 0.165]. Together, these panels show that introspection extends beyond one LLaMA checkpoint, but that probe quality, steering alignment, and report quality vary strongly across architectures, with the clearest generalization occurring in models that combine strong probes with correctly aligned steering responses.
