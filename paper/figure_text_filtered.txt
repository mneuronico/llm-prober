Fig. 1. Linear probe training recovers four interpretable internal directions in LLaMA-3.2-3B-Instruct and validates them on held-out evaluation prompts before any conversation-level analysis. Panels (A), (C), (E), and (G) show layer-wise Cohen's d sweeps for the happy-vs-sad, bored-vs-interested, distracted-vs-focused, and impulsive-vs-planning probes, respectively. The colored line is Cohen's d at each layer, the dashed vertical line marks the selected best layer, the colored point marks the maximum effect size, and the pale gray band marks the searched layer range (20%-80% of network depth). The best layers are 16, 14, 10, and 13, with best d values 3.3417, 1.6660, 1.9850, and 3.5950. For the sad-vs-happy and impulsive-vs-planning probes, displayed scores were sign-corrected after training so that larger plotted values always correspond to the intuitive pole. Panels (B), (D), (F), and (H) show the selected-layer score distributions for the same four concepts, with one score per held-out eval prompt. Using Welch's t-test on the 20 positive and 20 negative prompt scores, the two poles separate in all four cases: Happy vs Sad, d = 3.3417, p = 7.21e-13; Interested vs Bored, d = 1.6660, p = 9.45e-06; Focused vs Distracted, d = 1.9850, p = 5.73e-07; Impulsive vs Planning, d = 3.5950, p = 3.58e-13. All data in this figure come from the probe-training runs listed in `paper/dirs.txt`; evaluation prompts were distinct from training prompts, and the figure establishes the four probe directions later used to score conversation trajectories and self-reports.

Fig. 2. LLaMA-3.2-3B-Instruct shows concept drift during natural multi-turn conversations, but greedy numeric self-reports understate that drift; sampled decoding helps only modestly, whereas the logit-based self-report measure recovers a continuous signal that follows the internal-state changes. All panels are based on 40 independently generated 10-turn conversations. (A) Greedy integer self-reports (0-9) across turns for wellbeing, interest, focus, and impulsivity, with thin traces showing individual conversations and thick colored curves showing per-turn means; shaded bands are cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs across conversations. Using the mixed-effects model `rating ~ turn + (1 | conversation)`, greedy ratings show a small positive turn slope for wellbeing (0.0289 rating units/turn, p = 8.38e-04), a stronger positive slope for interest (0.1358, p = 4.98e-42), a weaker positive slope for focus (0.0217, p = 3.37e-06), and no reliable drift for impulsivity (0.0021, p = 0.199). (B) Probe scores from the trained concept directions, extracted at alpha = 0 and sign-corrected where needed, plotted with the same visualization conventions as in (A). The mixed-effects turn slopes are near zero for wellbeing (-0.00036, p = 0.642), positive for interest (0.00488, p = 4.12e-14), positive for focus (0.00164, p = 1.75e-10), and small but positive for impulsivity (0.00142, p = 0.00233). (C) Grouped bars show, for each turn and concept, how many distinct greedy responses out of the ten possible digits were used across the 40 conversations. Greedy outputs occupy only a narrow subset of the response space, averaging 3.9 unique values for wellbeing, 2.2 for interest, 1.4 for focus, and 1.1 for impulsivity. (D) Sampled token self-reports (temperature 0.8), plotted as in (A), remain discrete but less collapsed than greedy decoding. The same mixed-effects turn test shows positive drift for interest (slope = 0.1189, p = 2.26e-17) and focus (0.0326, p = 5.57e-05), but not for wellbeing (0.00227, p = 0.901) or impulsivity (0.0103, p = 0.545). (E) Logit-based self-reports, defined as the probability-weighted average over the logits of the digit tokens 0-9, plotted with the same bootstrap 95% CIs. The mixed-effects turn slopes are positive for wellbeing (0.0167, p = 3.06e-07), interest (0.0988, p = 2.77e-90), and focus (0.0422, p = 6.96e-48), and negative for impulsivity (-0.0127, p = 5.05e-07), showing a continuous drift signal aligned with the conceptual direction. (F) Shannon entropy of the self-report distribution at alpha = 0, using discrete entropy for greedy and sampled token ratings and 20-bin histogram entropy for the continuous logit-based ratings. Across all concepts the logit-based method is most informative, with entropies 3.68, 3.68, 3.27, and 3.11 bits for wellbeing, interest, focus, and impulsivity. (G) Scatterplots comparing sampled token ratings and logit-based ratings at alpha = 0, with colored points for individual observations and a black dashed OLS fit. The pooled linear `R^2` values are 0.1308, 0.3707, 0.2081, and 0.0668 for wellbeing, interest, focus, and impulsivity, and the mixed-effects calibration slopes are all positive: 0.0308 (p = 7.76e-04), 0.2725 (p = 1.91e-46), 0.1810 (p = 5.03e-19), and 0.0205 (p = 0.00706).

Fig. 3. Numeric self-reports track the probe-defined internal state, this relationship is already present at the first turn, and direct self-steering causally shifts self-reports in the predicted direction. Panels (A)-(J) use the LLaMA-3.2-3B experiments at alpha = 0 unless otherwise noted; probe scores and steering alphas are sign-corrected for wellbeing and impulsivity so that positive plotted values always mean more of the target concept. (A) Four scatterplots of polarity-corrected probe score versus logit-based self-report, with one point per conversation-turn observation and a black isotonic-regression fit. Pooled descriptive associations are positive for all four concepts, with Spearman rho values 0.6831, 0.7630, 0.3997, and 0.5090 and isotonic `R^2` values 0.4777, 0.5393, 0.1247, and 0.3142. Using the mixed-effects model `logit_report ~ probe + (1 | conversation)`, the probe slopes are positive in all four cases: 1.9842 (p = 4.38e-26), 6.3184 (p = 3.16e-54), 2.9385 (p = 8.24e-06), and 1.7907 (p = 4.59e-12). (B) Bar plots summarize isotonic `R^2` (left) and Spearman rho (right) for the true probe directions against equal-norm random-vector controls. Using paired t-tests on the per-conversation rho difference between the true and random probes, the true probes outperform the random controls for wellbeing (mean delta rho = 0.3103, p = 7.92e-04), focus (0.4133, p = 4.11e-05), and impulsivity (0.3230, p = 1.67e-04), whereas interest is descriptively higher but not significant by this paired test (0.1258, p = 0.114). (C) Turn-wise isotonic `R^2`, computed independently at each turn from the 40 conversations and plotted as colored lines with bootstrap 95% CIs. Introspection accuracy is already present at turn 1 and remains positive through turn 10 for every concept. (D) Turn-wise Spearman rho, again computed independently at each turn from the 40 conversations; shaded bands are bootstrap 95% CIs and the dotted horizontal line marks zero. After polarity correction, rho is positive on most turns and is already above zero at turn 1 for wellbeing, interest, and impulsivity. (CDii) First-versus-last-turn comparisons of the turn-wise metrics in (C) and (D), with cluster-bootstrap p values for the first-to-last difference. The strongest first-to-last improvement appears in wellbeing rho, from 0.5223 to 0.7856, delta = 0.2632, p = 0.049; the corresponding rho changes for interest, focus, and impulsivity are descriptive only, with p = 0.070, 0.327, and 1.000. (CDiii) Histograms of within-conversation changes in introspection quality across turns, where each histogram summarizes one concordance trend per conversation and the mixed-effects interaction test `logit_report ~ probe * turn + (1 | conversation)` is the primary inferential summary. The interaction term is significant for all four concepts, with p = 0.00151, < 1e-06, 6e-06, and 0.00374 for wellbeing, interest, focus, and impulsivity. (E) Mean logit-based self-report as a function of display-corrected steering alpha for the four self-steering experiments, with colored lines by concept, the vertical dotted line marking alpha = 0, and shaded bands showing cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs across conversations at each alpha. Using the mixed-effects model `logit_report ~ alpha + (1 | conversation)`, the alpha slopes are positive for all four concepts: 0.1864, 0.2464, 0.4025, and 0.0673, with p values effectively 0.0 at machine precision in all four cases. (F), (G), (H), and (I) show self-report drift through turns under five steering strengths for wellbeing, interest, focus, and impulsivity, respectively. Each colored curve is the per-turn mean logit self-report at one alpha, with cluster-bootstrap 95% CI shading across conversations. Temporal drift persists under self-steering in all four concepts, but the alpha dependence is not uniform across concepts. (J) Drift magnitude (last-first turn) versus display-corrected alpha, computed per conversation and summarized with cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs. Under the predeclared `LMM`-with-fallback rule, wellbeing drift increases slightly with alpha (LMM slope = 0.0118, p = 4.30e-06), interest and focus drift decrease with alpha (fallback mean per-conversation alpha slopes = -0.0949 and -0.1093, with p = 7.45e-14 and 1.57e-14 because the corresponding `LMM`s are singular), and impulsivity becomes more negative as alpha increases (LMM slope = -0.0284, p = 8.92e-53). Together these panels show that introspection is present, turn-resolved, direction-specific, and causally modulated by steering.

Fig. 4. A full 4 x 4 steering-by-measured-concept matrix reveals that introspection can be improved selectively by steering, and that the improvement can arise either from stronger internal-state signals, more informative self-reports, or both. All panels use the 4 x 4 LLaMA-3.2-3B steering analysis, with display alpha sign-corrected so that positive alpha always means more of the steering concept. (A)-(E) Heatmaps of isotonic `R^2` for every steering concept by measured concept at alpha = -4, -2, 0, +2, and +4. These panels are descriptive and visualize the full 80-condition screen. (F) Heatmap of the maximum increase in isotonic `R^2` relative to baseline (alpha = 0) for each steering/measured pair, with red boxes plus asterisks marking cells with cluster-bootstrap p < 0.05. Only two cells survive this criterion: `focus -> wellbeing`, with max increase 0.2987 at alpha = +4 (bootstrap p = 9.99e-04), and `impulsivity -> interest`, with max increase 0.0976 at alpha = +4 (p = 0.0120). (G) Isotonic `R^2` versus display-corrected alpha for the two significant cells, plotted as colored curves with bootstrap 95% CIs. For `impulsivity -> interest`, the conversation-level `R^2` mixed-effects slope is positive (0.0177, p = 2.95e-10). For `focus -> wellbeing`, the corresponding `LMM` is singular, so the predeclared fallback is the per-conversation alpha-correlation test, which remains positive (mean rho = 0.4625, p = 1.57e-05). (H) Spearman rho versus display-corrected alpha for the same two cells, again with bootstrap 95% CIs. For `impulsivity -> interest`, the conversation-level rho mixed-effects slope is positive (0.0204, p = 1.60e-12). For `focus -> wellbeing`, the `LMM` is again singular, and the fallback per-conversation alpha-correlation test is positive (mean rho = 0.5128, p = 1.20e-06). (I) Probe-score drift across turns for the `focus -> wellbeing` condition, with one alpha-colored curve per steering strength and bootstrap 95% CIs across conversations. Negative alphas flatten or eliminate the drift, whereas positive alphas amplify it. (J) The same probe-drift analysis for `impulsivity -> interest`. Here the internal-state drift remains positive at all alphas and varies more modestly. (K) Variance of the logit self-report as a function of display alpha for the two significant cells, plotted with bootstrap 95% CIs. The primary variance test is the OLS coefficient in `log(variance) ~ alpha + turn_factor`: variance rises with alpha in both conditions, with alpha coefficient 0.2185 (p = 8.52e-12) for `focus -> wellbeing` and 0.0674 (p = 3.17e-08) for `impulsivity -> interest`. (L) Mean probe-score drift magnitude (last-first turn) versus display alpha for the same two cells, with points and error bars showing bootstrap 95% CIs from per-conversation drift values. The mixed-effects alpha slope is positive in both cases, 0.01726 for `focus -> wellbeing` (p = 2.69e-78) and 0.00493 for `impulsivity -> interest` (p = 9.28e-78). Taken together, panels (I)-(L) show that both latent-state drift and report-side variability improve in both significant steering conditions, with a larger latent-state effect in `focus -> wellbeing`.

Fig. 5. The main findings generalize unevenly across model scales and families: introspection becomes much stronger in larger LLaMA models for some validated probes, concept drift is visible across scales, and cross-family replication is much stronger in Qwen than in Gemma. (A) Heatmap of probe quality across LLaMA 1B, 3B, and 8B, where each cell reports the maximum layer-wise Cohen's d for one concept pair. Probe quality varies across both scales and concepts. (B) Isotonic `R^2` versus model size at alpha = 0. Introspection rises descriptively with size for wellbeing (0.2587 -> 0.4520 -> 0.9022) and interest (0.0527 -> 0.6572 -> 0.9313), but remains weak for focus and impulsivity; this panel is treated as descriptive under the current aggregate-by-size output. (Bii) Spearman rho versus model size for the same runs, using polarity-corrected probe scores. The same descriptive pattern holds in rank space, again with clear gains for wellbeing and interest but not for focus or impulsivity. (Biii) Scatterplots for the two highest-performing LLaMA 8B concepts, wellbeing and interest, with one point per conversation-turn observation and black isotonic fits. These two probes achieve near-ceiling descriptive introspection, with rho = 0.9335 and isotonic `R^2` = 0.9022 for wellbeing and rho = 0.9580 and `R^2` = 0.9313 for interest. Using the mixed-effects model `logit_report ~ probe + (1 | conversation)`, the probe slopes are 8.3685 for wellbeing and 4.3585 for interest, with p values under machine precision in both cases. (C) Left: three subpanels, one for each LLaMA size, plotting mean logit self-report versus display-corrected steering alpha for all four concepts; colored lines indicate concepts and shaded bands are cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs across conversations. Right: a heatmap of pooled Spearman rho(alpha, self-report), shown descriptively. Using the mixed-effects model `logit_report ~ alpha + (1 | conversation)`, all twelve concept-size alpha slopes differ from zero; the notable sign reversals are 1B focus (slope = -0.6166, p under machine precision) and 8B impulsivity (slope = -0.0536, p = 1.77e-75), whereas the other ten slopes are positive. (D) Mean isotonic `R^2` across the subset of concept/model pairs whose steering-response sign passed the validation filter, shown as a bar chart with bootstrap 95% CIs. Under this filter, mean validated `R^2` increases from 0.1192 in LLaMA 1B to 0.3692 in 3B and 0.6136 in 8B. (E) Probe-score drift across turns for the wellbeing direction in LLaMA 1B, 3B, and 8B, with one line per size and cluster-bootstrap 95% CI shading. The mixed-effects turn slopes are positive at all three scales, 0.00591, 0.00479, and 0.01326, with p = 2.10e-22, 1.12e-10, and 5.22e-73. (Eii) Bar plot of the same probe-drift magnitudes, where bars show the mean per-conversation drift and error bars show bootstrap 95% CIs. A direct OLS test on per-conversation drifts versus log model size is positive (slope = 0.0413, p = 1.01e-06), supporting the claim that probe drift increases with scale in this example. (F) Logit self-report drift across turns for the same happy-vs-sad example, again with one line per size and cluster-bootstrap 95% CI shading. The mixed-effects turn slopes are positive at every scale, 0.1594, 0.0381, and 0.1411, with p = 1.13e-56, 4.01e-30, and 8.85e-128, but the effect is not monotonic with size. (Fii) Bar plot of the same report-drift magnitudes with bootstrap 95% CIs. The direct OLS size-trend test on per-conversation drifts is negative rather than positive (slope = -0.2318, p = 0.0314), so this panel does not support a simple report-drift-increases-with-scale claim. (G) Normalized layer-sweep comparison for the wellbeing probe in Gemma 3 4B and Qwen 2.5 7B, with the x axis scaled from 0 to 1 to align models of different depths. Qwen yields the much stronger probe, best d = 3.544 at normalized layer 0.741, than Gemma, best d = 1.815 at normalized layer 0.455. (H) Logit self-report drift through turns for wellbeing in Gemma and Qwen, with colored lines by family and cluster-bootstrap 95% CI shading. Using the mixed-effects model `rating ~ turn + (1 | conversation)`, Gemma shows a positive report drift with slope 0.0555 (p = 9.83e-34) and Qwen also shows a smaller positive slope, 0.0261 (p = 1.08e-04). (I) Scatterplot of probe score versus logit self-report for Gemma, with a black isotonic fit. Descriptively, rho = 0.2808 and isotonic `R^2` = 0.1141; inferentially, the mixed-effects probe slope is 0.00948 (p = 1.33e-13). (J) The corresponding scatterplot for Qwen. Descriptively, rho = 0.4890 and isotonic `R^2` = 0.7566; inferentially, the mixed-effects probe slope is 0.27995 (p = 1.19e-84). (K) Turn-wise isotonic `R^2` for Gemma and Qwen on wellbeing, computed independently at each turn from the 40 conversations and shown with bootstrap 95% CIs. Using the prespecified cluster-bootstrap first-to-last comparison, Gemma shows no reliable change (delta = +0.0904, p = 0.567), whereas Qwen declines strongly from turn 1 to turn 10 (delta = -0.4418, p = 0.001). (L) Turn-wise Spearman rho for the same comparison; shaded bands are bootstrap 95% CIs and the dotted horizontal line marks zero. The same first-to-last bootstrap test is not significant for either model, with delta = +0.3313, p = 0.154 for Gemma and delta = -0.2248, p = 0.165 for Qwen. Together, these panels show that introspection extends beyond one LLaMA checkpoint, but that probe quality, steering alignment, and report quality vary strongly across architectures, with the clearest generalization occurring in models that combine strong probes with correctly aligned steering responses.
