Fig. 1. Linear probes recover four interpretable internal directions in LLaMA-3.2-3B-Instruct. (A), (C), (E), and (G) show layer-wise Cohen's d sweeps for the sad-vs-happy (from now on, 'wellbeing'), bored-vs-interested (from now on, 'interest'), distracted-vs-focused (from now on, 'focus'), and planning-vs-impulsive (from now on, 'impulsivity') probes; dashed lines mark the selected layers and the gray band marks the searched layer range. The best layers are 16, 14, 10, and 13, with peak d values 3.34, 1.67, 1.99, and 3.60. For the wellbeing and impulsivity probes, scores were sign-corrected so that larger values align with larger self-reported values in later experiments. (B), (D), (F), and (H) show the selected-layer score distributions on held-out evaluation prompts; boxplots show prompt-level scores. The two poles separate in all four cases (Welch's t-test: wellbeing, d = 3.34, p = 7.21e-13; interest, d = 1.67, p = 9.45e-06; focus, d = 1.99, p = 5.73e-07; impulsivity, d = 3.60, p = 3.58e-13).

Fig. 2. Internal-state drift is tracked by numeric self-reports of the same concept. All panels use 40 ten-turn conversations; shaded bands denote cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs across conversations. (A) Greedy integer self-reports across turns, with thin lines showing individual conversations and thick lines showing per-turn means. Greedy ratings are largely collapsed, with clear positive drift only for interest (mixed-effects turn slope = 0.1358, p = 4.98e-42) and weaker positive drift for wellbeing and focus (slopes = 0.0289 and 0.0217, p = 8.38e-04 and 3.37e-06); impulsivity does not drift reliably (slope = 0.0021, p = 0.199). (B) Probe scores from the trained concept directions across turns. Probe scores drift strongly for interest and focus (slopes = 0.00488 and 0.00164, p = 4.12e-14 and 1.75e-10), are flat for wellbeing (slope = -0.00036, p = 0.642), and show only a small positive effect for impulsivity (slope = 0.00142, p = 0.00233). (C) Average number of distinct greedy responses used for each concept across the 40 conversations, averaging across turns. Greedy outputs occupy only a narrow subset of the 0-9 response space, especially for focus and impulsivity. (D) Logit-based self-reports, defined as the probability-weighted average over the digit-token logits. Unlike greedy or sampled token outputs, the logit-based measure shows robust drift in all four concepts, with positive slopes for wellbeing, interest, and focus and a negative slope for impulsivity (all p < 1e-06). (E) Shannon entropy of the self-report distribution. The logit-based method is most informative in all four concepts.

Appendix Fig. 2. Supplementary self-report analyses for Fig. 2. All panels use the same 40 ten-turn conversations. (A) Number of distinct greedy responses used at each turn across the 40 conversations. Greedy outputs occupy only a narrow subset of the 0-9 response space, especially for focus and impulsivity. (B) Sampled token self-reports (temperature 0.8), plotted as in Fig. 2(A). Sampling reduces collapse but remains discrete and noisy; only interest and focus show reliable positive turn slopes (0.1189 and 0.0326, p = 2.26e-17 and 5.57e-05). (C) Logit-based ratings versus sampled token ratings; dashed lines show OLS fits. The two measures are positively related in all four concepts (mixed-effects calibration slopes range from 0.0205 to 0.2725, all p < 0.01).

Fig. 3. Self-reports track the probe-defined internal state from the first turn, and direct self-steering shifts self-reports causally in the predicted direction. (A) Polarity-corrected probe score versus logit-based self-report, with one point per conversation-turn observation and black isotonic fits. Descriptive associations are positive for all four concepts (pooled rho = 0.40-0.76; isotonic R^2 = 0.12-0.54), and mixed-effects probe slopes are positive in all cases (all p < 1e-05). (B) Isotonic R^2 (left) and Spearman rho (right) for the true probes versus equal-norm random-vector controls; error bars denote cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs for both true and control bars. In matched-conversation cluster-bootstrap comparisons of the pooled plotted metrics, the true probes outperform random controls for all four concepts (delta rho = 0.792, 0.665, 0.230, and 0.536; p = 0.002, 0.002, 0.028, and 0.002; delta R^2 = 0.440, 0.437, 0.093, and 0.200; p = 0.002, 0.002, 0.022, and 0.042). (C) Turn-wise isotonic R^2, computed independently at each turn and shown in separate concept panels; shaded bands denote bootstrap 95% CIs. (D) Turn-wise Spearman rho, computed independently at each turn and shown in separate concept panels; shaded bands denote bootstrap 95% CIs. Introspection is already present at turn 1 and remains positive through turn 10. In a mixed-effects interaction test (logit_report ~ probe * turn + (1 | conversation)) the interaction is significant for all four concepts (p < 0.01), showing that probe-report coupling (introspection) changes over time. From turn 1 to turn 10, turnwise R^2 changes by +0.309, +0.270, +0.170, and -0.278, and turnwise rho changes by +0.263, +0.245, +0.215, and -0.237, for wellbeing, interest, focus, and impulsivity respectively. (E) Combined version of the turn-wise isotonic R^2 curves, with all four concepts shown in the same panel and lighter error bands. (F) Combined version of the turn-wise Spearman rho curves, with all four concepts shown in the same panel and lighter error bands. (G) Mean logit-based self-report versus steering alpha; shaded bands denote cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs. Steering shifts self-report monotonically in the expected direction for all four concepts (mixed-effects alpha slopes 0.0673-0.4025, all p < 1e-12). (H) Drift magnitude (last minus first turn) versus display-corrected alpha, with cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs. Wellbeing drift becomes slightly more positive with alpha (LMM slope = 0.0118, p = 4.30e-06), impulsivity drift becomes more negative with alpha (LMM slope = -0.0284, p = 8.92e-53), and interest and focus drift decrease with alpha (fallback per-conversation slope means = -0.0949 and -0.1093; p = 7.45e-14 and 1.57e-14, because the corresponding LMMs are singular).

Fig. 4. Steering one concept can selectively improve introspection for another. (A) Maximum increase in isotonic R^2 relative to alpha = 0 for each steering/measured pair; red boxes mark cells with significant cluster-bootstrap improvements. Only `focus -> wellbeing` and `impulsivity -> interest` survive this criterion (delta R^2 = 0.2987, p = 9.99e-04; delta R^2 = 0.0976, p = 0.0120). (B) Isotonic R^2 and (C) Spearman rho versus display-corrected alpha for those two significant cells; shaded bands denote bootstrap 95% CIs. Both metrics increase with alpha in both cells. For `impulsivity -> interest`, the conversation-level mixed-effects alpha slopes are positive for both R^2 and rho (0.0177 and 0.0204; p = 2.95e-10 and 1.60e-12). For `focus -> wellbeing`, the corresponding LMMs are singular, but the fallback per-conversation alpha-correlation tests remain positive (mean rho = 0.4625 and 0.5128; p = 1.57e-05 and 1.20e-06). (D) Mean probe-drift magnitude versus display alpha, with cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs shown as shaded bands. Probe-drift magnitude also increases with alpha in both cells (mixed-effects slopes = 0.01726 and 0.00493; p = 2.69e-78 and 9.28e-78), indicating that latent-state change contributes to the improvement. (E) Variance of the logit self-report versus display alpha; shaded bands denote bootstrap 95% CIs. Report variance also increases with alpha in both significant cells (OLS alpha coefficient = 0.2185, p = 8.52e-12 for `focus -> wellbeing`; 0.0674, p = 3.17e-08 for `impulsivity -> interest`), indicating that report-side variability contributes as well.

Fig. 5. Results generalize unevenly across model scales and families: introspection becomes much stronger in larger LLaMA models for some probes, concept drift is visible across scales, and cross-family replication is stronger in Qwen than in Gemma. (A) Isotonic R^2 versus model size. Introspection increases strongly with size for wellbeing and interest, but remains weak for focus and impulsivity. (B) Spearman rho versus model size. The same descriptive pattern holds in rank space. (C) Probe score versus logit self-report for the two strongest LLaMA 8B probes, wellbeing and interest, with black isotonic fits. Both show near-ceiling introspection (rho = 0.9335 and 0.9580; isotonic R^2 = 0.9022 and 0.9313), and mixed-effects probe slopes are strongly positive in both cases (p < 1e-10). (D) Heatmap of the pooled Spearman rho(alpha, self-report) from the steering analysis across concept and LLaMA size, shown descriptively. (E) Mean isotonic R^2 across the subset of concept-model pairs that pass the steering-sign validation filter, with bootstrap 95% CIs. Mean validated R^2 increases from 1B to 3B to 8B (0.119, 0.369, 0.614), and a bootstrap test on the slope of size-wise means versus log model size is positive but only marginally significant (mean slope = 0.237, p = 0.072). (F) Probe-score drift across turns for the wellbeing direction in 1B, 3B, and 8B; shaded bands denote cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs. Mixed-effects turn slopes are positive at all three scales (0.00591, 0.00479, and 0.01326; p = 2.10e-22, 1.12e-10, and 5.22e-73). (G) Mean probe-drift magnitude versus model size, with bootstrap 95% CIs shown as shaded bands. Under the same bootstrap size-slope analysis, probe-drift magnitude increases with scale (mean slope vs log size = 0.0413, 95% CI = [0.0261, 0.0565], p < 2e-04). (H) Logit self-report drift across turns for the same happy-vs-sad example; shaded bands denote cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs. Turn slopes are positive at all three scales, but the effect is not monotonic with size. (I) Mean report-drift magnitude versus model size, with bootstrap 95% CIs shown as shaded bands. Unlike probe drift, report drift does not increase with scale; under the same bootstrap size-slope analysis, the slope is negative (mean slope = -0.2333, 95% CI = [-0.4344, -0.0326], two-sided p = 0.023). (J) Logit self-report drift through turns for wellbeing in Gemma and Qwen; shaded bands denote cluster-bootstrap 95% CIs. Mixed-effects turn slopes are positive in both families, but larger in Gemma than in Qwen (0.0555, p = 9.83e-34 vs 0.0261, p = 1.08e-04). (K) Probe score versus logit self-report for Gemma and (L) for Qwen, with black isotonic fits. Qwen shows stronger introspection than Gemma (rho = 0.4890 vs 0.2808; isotonic R^2 = 0.7566 vs 0.1141), although mixed-effects probe slopes are positive in both cases. (M) Turn-wise isotonic R^2 and (N) turn-wise Spearman rho for the same cross-family comparison; shaded bands denote bootstrap 95% CIs. Qwen shows a strong first-to-last decline in turnwise R^2 (delta = -0.4418, cluster-bootstrap p = 0.001), whereas the corresponding first-to-last rho changes are not significant in either family.
