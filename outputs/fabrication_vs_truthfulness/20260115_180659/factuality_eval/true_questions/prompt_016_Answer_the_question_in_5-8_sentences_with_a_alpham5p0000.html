The Doppler effect is a phenomenon in physics where the frequency of a wave changes when the source of the wave and the observer are moving relative to each other. In the case of sound waves, the frequency change occurs when the source of the sound and the observer are moving relative to each other. When the source is moving towards the observer, the frequency increases (doppler shift). When the source is moving away from the observer, the frequency decreases. This is commonly observed in traffic, where a siren of a moving car is heard as a higher pitch if it is approaching and as a lower pitch if it is moving away.
