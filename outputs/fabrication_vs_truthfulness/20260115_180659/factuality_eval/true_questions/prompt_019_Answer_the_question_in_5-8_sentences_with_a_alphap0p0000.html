Founded in 1863 by Henry Dunant, the movement is based on the principles of humanity, impartiality, neutrality, and independence. The Red Cross movement consists of 192 national societies and 7 international organizations, working together to provide a wide range of services, including blood donation, first aid training, and disaster response. The Red Cross also provides humanitarian aid to refugees, migrants, and people affected by conflicts and natural disasters. In addition, the movement promotes international cooperation, advocacy, and education to promote human rights, dignity, and well-being.
