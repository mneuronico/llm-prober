The Doppler effect is a fundamental concept in physics that describes the change in frequency or pitch that occurs when an observer is moving relative to a source of sound waves. In the context of sound waves, the Doppler effect is commonly observed in situations where a moving object, such as a car or a train, produces a change in pitch as it approaches or recedes from an observer. When a car approaches an observer, the pitch of the sound it produces appears to increase, while when it recedes, the pitch appears to decrease. This phenomenon is also observed in sirens of emergency vehicles, which sound higher in pitch when approaching and lower in pitch when receding from an observer.
