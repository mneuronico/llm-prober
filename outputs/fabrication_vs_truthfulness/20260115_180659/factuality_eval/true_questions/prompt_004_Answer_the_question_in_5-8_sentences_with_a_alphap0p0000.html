The theory of natural selection, also known as Darwinian evolution, proposes that organisms with favorable traits are more likely to survive and reproduce, passing those traits on to their offspring. This process occurs over generations, leading to the evolution of species. The theory was first proposed by Charles Darwin in his 1859 book "On the Origin of Species." The evidence supporting natural selection comes from various fields, including biology, genetics, and paleontology. One key piece of evidence is the observation of adaptations in different species. Additionally, genetic studies have confirmed that the genetic variations underlying adaptations are often present in populations before the adaptation occurs.
