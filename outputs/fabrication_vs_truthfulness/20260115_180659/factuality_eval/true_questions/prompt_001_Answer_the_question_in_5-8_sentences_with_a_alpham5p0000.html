The Haber-Bosch process is a method for producing ammonia (NH3) from nitrogen (N2) and hydrogen (H2) using a catalyst. It involves the reaction of nitrogen and hydrogen in the presence of a catalyst, typically iron (Fe) or iron alloy, in a process called synthesis. The reaction is as follows: N2 + 3H2 → 2NH3. The reaction occurs when nitrogen and hydrogen are exposed to a high temperature (around 450-500°C) and pressure (around 100-150 atmospheres) in the presence of a catalyst. The catalyst facilitates the reaction by lowering the activation energy, allowing the reaction to occur more easily. The process is also known as Haber process.
