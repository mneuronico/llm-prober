A lunar eclipse occurs when the Earth passes between the sun and the moon, blocking the sunlight that normally reflects off the moon's surface. This alignment is known as a syzygy, which is a rare occurrence of three celestial bodies aligning in a straight line. The Earth, moon, and sun must be in a straight line, with the Earth positioned between the sun and the moon, for a lunar eclipse to occur. There are three types of lunar eclipses: penumbral, partial, and total, each occurring when the Earth's shadow falls on the moon in different ways.
