A hydroelectric dam generates electricity from the energy of moving water. The process involves water from a river or reservoir flowing through a penstock (a large pipe) into a turbine. The water flows through the turbine, causing it to spin. The spinning turbine is connected to a generator, which converts the mechanical energy of the spinning turbine into electrical energy. The electrical energy is then transmitted to a substation and distributed to power utilities, where it is transmitted to homes and businesses. Here's a more detailed explanation: 1.
