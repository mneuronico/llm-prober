The metric system, also known as the International System of Units (SI), is a decimal-based system of measurement that originated in France during the French Revolution in the late 18th century. It was developed to provide a consistent and logical system of measurement, replacing the various traditional systems of measurement in use at the time. The metric system is based on the concept of the meter for length, the liter for volume, and the second for time, with other units derived from these fundamental units. The system's use of the decimal system and its logical structure made it an attractive choice for international use. In 1960, the 11th General Conference on Weights and Measures (CGPM) adopted the metric system as the global standard for measurement, making it the official system of measurement for science, technology, and commerce.
