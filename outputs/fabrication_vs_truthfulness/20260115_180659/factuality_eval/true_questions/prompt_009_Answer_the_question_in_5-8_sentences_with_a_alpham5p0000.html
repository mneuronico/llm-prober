Antibiotic resistance is a process where bacteria develop the ability to withstand the effects of antibiotics, rendering them ineffective. This can happen through genetic mutation, where bacteria can acquire a mutation in a gene that codes for a protein involved in bacterial cell wall synthesis, making it resistant to antibiotics. Another way is through acquiring resistance genes from other bacteria, which can transfer genes through a process called transformation. Antibiotic resistance can also develop through exposure to antibiotics, leading to genetic mutations in bacteria that can survive and multiply. Practices that accelerate antibiotic resistance include: * Overuse or misuse of antibiotics: Using antibiotics for infections when they are not needed or using them for longer than necessary increases the chance of developing resistance. * Overuse of antibiotics in agriculture: Antibiotics are often used in agriculture to promote growth and to prevent disease in animals.
