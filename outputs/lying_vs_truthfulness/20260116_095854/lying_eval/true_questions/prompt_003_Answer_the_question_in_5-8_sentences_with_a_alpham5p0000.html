A lunar eclipse occurs when the Earth comes between the Sun and the Moon, blocking the sunlight that normally reaches the Moon. This alignment is known as a synergetic event, where the Earth, Moon, and Sun are nearly in a straight line. There are three types of lunar eclipses: penumbral, partial, and total. A penumbral lunar eclipse occurs when the Moon passes through the Earth's penumbra (partial shadow), resulting in a gradual decrease in brightness. A partial lunar eclipse occurs when the Moon passes through the Earth's partial shadow, causing a portion of the Moon to darken. A total lunar eclipse occurs when the Moon passes through the Earth's umbra (complete shadow), causing the entire Moon to darken and take on a reddish hue, known as a "blood moon."
