The Doppler effect is the change in frequency of sound waves when the source of the sound and the observer are moving relative to each other. When the source of the sound is moving towards the observer, the frequency of the sound waves increases (frequency increases). When the source of the sound is moving away from the observer, the frequency of the sound waves decreases (frequency decreases). This is also known as the Doppler effect, which is a result of the relative motion between the source of the sound and the observer. The Doppler effect can be observed in two main situations: 1. When a moving object, such as a vehicle, approaches a stationary observer, the frequency of the sound waves increases, and the frequency of the sound waves emitted by a source of sound increases. 2. When a moving object, such as a vehicle, moves away from a stationary observer, the frequency of the sound waves decreases. The Doppler effect is commonly observed in various situations
