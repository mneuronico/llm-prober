Antibiotic resistance develops in bacteria through a process where bacteria adapt to survive exposure to antibiotics. This occurs when bacteria that are susceptible to antibiotics multiply and die, leaving behind bacteria that are resistant to the antibiotic. Resistance can also arise from genetic mutations, gene transfer between bacteria, and horizontal gene transfer from bacteria to other organisms. The selection pressure exerted by antibiotics accelerates the development of resistance. Overuse and misuse of antibiotics are significant factors that contribute to the development of resistance. When antibiotics are used excessively or incorrectly, they can select for resistant bacteria, which can then multiply and spread. This can lead to the spread of resistant bacteria, increasing the risk of infection and reducing the effectiveness of antibiotics.
