What is the purpose of a control group in experiments?
assistant
The primary purpose of a control group in experiments is to provide a baseline or standard against which the outcomes of the experimental group can be compared. A control group is a group of participants who receive a placebo, a neutral treatment, or no treatment at all, and are not exposed to the experimental treatment or intervention being tested.
