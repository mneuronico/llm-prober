What is the purpose of a control group in experiments?
assistant
The primary purpose of a control group in experiments is to provide a baseline or a standard against which the results of the experimental group can be compared. The control group serves as a comparison group that does not receive the treatment or intervention being tested, allowing researchers to isolate the effects of the treatment and determine its validity.
